## I. Cities

### 1. Berlin, Germany
- **ID**: `loc-berlin`
- **Type**: City
- **Country**: Germany
- **Significance**: Capital of Berlin School electronic music. Home to Tangerine Dream, Klaus Schulze, Conrad Schnitzler, Ash Ra Tempel. The Zodiak Free Arts Lab (1968) was crucial experimental venue.
- **Active Period**: 1967-present
- **Connected Persons**: Edgar Froese, Klaus Schulze, Conrad Schnitzler, Manuel Göttsching
- **Connected Entities**: Tangerine Dream, Zodiak Free Arts Lab

### 2. Düsseldorf, Germany
- **ID**: `loc-dusseldorf`
- **Type**: City
- **Country**: Germany
- **Significance**: Home of Kraftwerk and birthplace of electronic pop. Also base for Neu! and Michael Rother. Joseph Beuys taught at the art academy.
- **Active Period**: 1970-present
- **Connected Persons**: Ralf Hütter, Florian Schneider, Michael Rother, Klaus Dinger
- **Connected Entities**: Kraftwerk, Neu!

### 3. Cologne, Germany
- **ID**: `loc-cologne`
- **Type**: City
- **Country**: Germany
- **Significance**: Site of WDR electronic music studio where Stockhausen worked. Also location (nearby) of Conny Plank's studio. Major center of German electronic experimentation.
- **Active Period**: 1951-1980s
- **Connected Persons**: Karlheinz Stockhausen, Conny Plank
- **Connected Entities**: WDR Studio for Electronic Music

### 4. Forst, Germany
- **ID**: `loc-forst`
- **Type**: Town/Village
- **Country**: Germany
- **Significance**: Small town where Cluster (Roedelius and Moebius) established their studio. Site of Harmonia sessions and Brian Eno's crucial 1976 visit.
- **Active Period**: 1973-1990s
- **Connected Persons**: Hans-Joachim Roedelius, Dieter Moebius, Michael Rother, Brian Eno
- **Connected Entities**: Cluster, Harmonia

### 5. Paris, France
- **ID**: `loc-paris`
- **Type**: City
- **Country**: France
- **Significance**: Birthplace of musique concrète at RTF studios. Home to GRM (Groupe de Recherches Musicales). Also where Erik Satie developed furniture music concept and Maurice Martenot invented the ondes Martenot.
- **Active Period**: 1910s-present
- **Connected Persons**: Erik Satie, Pierre Schaeffer, Pierre Henry, François Bayle, Maurice Martenot
- **Connected Entities**: GRM, RTF/ORTF

### 6. London, UK
- **ID**: `loc-london`
- **Type**: City
- **Country**: United Kingdom
- **Significance**: Home of BBC Radiophonic Workshop, EMS synthesizers, and British experimental scene. Base for Brian Eno, Robert Fripp, Gavin Bryars. Art schools (Ipswich, Winchester nearby) shaped Eno's approach.
- **Active Period**: 1960s-present
- **Connected Persons**: Brian Eno, Robert Fripp, Delia Derbyshire, Peter Zinovieff, David Cockerell, Tristram Cary, Gavin Bryars, Michael Nyman
- **Connected Entities**: BBC Radiophonic Workshop, EMS, Obscure Records

### 7. New York City, USA
- **ID**: `loc-new-york`
- **Type**: City
- **Country**: United States
- **Significance**: Center of American avant-garde and minimalism. Home to John Cage, Morton Feldman, La Monte Young (Theatre of Eternal Music), Steve Reich, Philip Glass, Tony Conrad. Also Robert Moog's early base.
- **Active Period**: 1950s-present
- **Connected Persons**: John Cage, Morton Feldman, La Monte Young, Tony Conrad, Steve Reich, Philip Glass, David Tudor, Robert Moog, Leon Theremin (exile period), Raymond Scott
- **Connected Entities**: Theatre of Eternal Music, New York School

### 8. San Francisco Bay Area, USA
- **ID**: `loc-san-francisco`
- **Type**: Metropolitan area
- **Country**: United States
- **Significance**: Home of San Francisco Tape Music Center (Pauline Oliveros, Morton Subotnick). Don Buchla developed instruments here. Terry Riley based here. Center of West Coast experimental music.
- **Active Period**: 1960s-1970s
- **Connected Persons**: Pauline Oliveros, Terry Riley, Don Buchla, Steve Reich (early career)
- **Connected Entities**: San Francisco Tape Music Center, Mills College

### 9. Kingston, Jamaica
- **ID**: `loc-kingston`
- **Type**: City
- **Country**: Jamaica
- **Significance**: Birthplace of dub music. King Tubby's studio and Lee "Scratch" Perry's Black Ark developed "studio as instrument" techniques that influenced ambient production.
- **Active Period**: 1968-1979
- **Connected Persons**: King Tubby, Lee "Scratch" Perry
- **Connected Studios**: King Tubby's Studio, Black Ark

### 10. Tokyo, Japan
- **ID**: `loc-tokyo`
- **Type**: City
- **Country**: Japan
- **Significance**: Center of Japanese electronic and ambient music. Home to YMO (Yellow Magic Orchestra) and kankyō ongaku (environmental music) movement.
- **Active Period**: 1978-present
- **Connected Persons**: Haruomi Hosono, Ryuichi Sakamoto, Hiroshi Yoshimura

### 11. Milan, Italy
- **ID**: `loc-milan`
- **Type**: City
- **Country**: Italy
- **Significance**: Base of Italian Futurism where Luigi Russolo developed intonarumori instruments and wrote *The Art of Noises*.
- **Active Period**: 1909-1920s
- **Connected Persons**: Luigi Russolo

### 12. Hamilton, Ontario, Canada
- **ID**: `loc-hamilton`
- **Type**: City
- **Country**: Canada
- **Significance**: Location of Daniel Lanois's Grant Avenue Studio, important ambient recording location. Base for Lanois before his move to larger productions.
- **Active Period**: 1970s-1980s
- **Connected Persons**: Daniel Lanois
- **Connected Studios**: Grant Avenue Studio

## II. Studios

### 13. Conny Plank's Studio (Conny's Studio)
- **ID**: `loc-conny-studio`
- **Type**: Recording studio
- **Location**: Near Cologne, Germany (converted farmhouse in Wolperath)
- **Significance**: Crucible of German electronic music. Plank produced Kraftwerk, Cluster, Neu!, Harmonia, and facilitated Eno collaborations here. The studio itself was treated as instrument.
- **Active Period**: 1974-1987
- **Connected Persons**: Conny Plank, Brian Eno, Cluster, Kraftwerk, Neu!, Harmonia
- **Notes**: Plank's death in 1987 ended this era

### 14. GRM (Groupe de Recherches Musicales)
- **ID**: `loc-grm`
- **Type**: Studio/Institution
- **Location**: Paris, France (RTF/ORTF/Radio France)
- **Significance**: First electroacoustic music studio, founded by Pierre Schaeffer. Birthplace of musique concrète. Directed by François Bayle 1966-1997. Institutionalized experimental electronic music.
- **Active Period**: 1958-present (predecessor RTF studio from 1948)
- **Connected Persons**: Pierre Schaeffer, Pierre Henry, François Bayle, Karlheinz Stockhausen (visitor 1952)

### 15. WDR Studio for Electronic Music
- **ID**: `loc-wdr-studio`
- **Type**: Studio
- **Location**: Cologne, Germany
- **Significance**: Pioneering electronic music studio where Stockhausen created landmark works. Established as counterpoint to Paris musique concrète—focused on purely electronic sound generation.
- **Active Period**: 1951-2000 (most active 1951-1970s)
- **Connected Persons**: Karlheinz Stockhausen

### 16. BBC Radiophonic Workshop
- **ID**: `loc-bbc-radiophonic`
- **Type**: Studio/Department
- **Location**: Maida Vale, London, UK
- **Significance**: BBC department creating electronic sound for TV and radio. Delia Derbyshire realized Doctor Who theme here. Pioneered tape manipulation for mass media.
- **Active Period**: 1958-1998
- **Connected Persons**: Delia Derbyshire, Tristram Cary
- **Notes**: Created sounds for Doctor Who, educational programs, etc.

### 17. King Tubby's Studio (Hometown Hi-Fi)
- **ID**: `loc-king-tubby-studio`
- **Type**: Studio
- **Location**: Kingston, Jamaica
- **Significance**: Small home studio where King Tubby invented dub mixing techniques—stripping tracks, adding delay and reverb, creating spatial depth. Birthplace of remix culture.
- **Active Period**: 1971-1989
- **Connected Persons**: King Tubby

### 18. Black Ark Studio
- **ID**: `loc-black-ark`
- **Type**: Studio
- **Location**: Kingston, Jamaica
- **Significance**: Lee "Scratch" Perry's legendary studio where he pushed dub into experimental territory. Used unconventional techniques, found sounds, and spiritual practices. Burned down 1979.
- **Active Period**: 1973-1979
- **Connected Persons**: Lee "Scratch" Perry
- **Notes**: Perry burned it down in 1979; mystical/unstable environment

### 19. Kling Klang Studio
- **ID**: `loc-kling-klang`
- **Type**: Studio
- **Location**: Düsseldorf, Germany
- **Significance**: Kraftwerk's private studio where they developed their signature sound independently after working with Conny Plank. Became hermetically sealed creative environment.
- **Active Period**: 1970-present
- **Connected Persons**: Ralf Hütter, Florian Schneider

### 20. Grant Avenue Studio
- **ID**: `loc-grant-avenue`
- **Type**: Studio
- **Location**: Hamilton, Ontario, Canada
- **Significance**: Daniel Lanois's studio where he developed his production aesthetic. Ambient recordings including work with Eno made here before Lanois went on to produce U2, etc.
- **Active Period**: 1970s-1980s
- **Connected Persons**: Daniel Lanois, Brian Eno

### 21. EMS Studio (Putney)
- **ID**: `loc-ems-studio`
- **Type**: Studio/Workshop
- **Location**: Putney, London, UK (Peter Zinovieff's house)
- **Significance**: Home studio where EMS synthesizers were developed. Also pioneered early computer music in Britain.
- **Active Period**: 1966-1979
- **Connected Persons**: Peter Zinovieff, David Cockerell, Tristram Cary

### 22. Hansa Studio (by the Wall)
- **ID**: `loc-hansa`
- **Type**: Studio
- **Location**: Berlin, Germany (near Berlin Wall)
- **Significance**: Studio where David Bowie recorded *Low* and *Heroes* with Eno. Its location near the Wall contributed to the albums' atmosphere.
- **Active Period**: 1970s-present
- **Connected Persons**: Brian Eno, David Bowie (not in enumeration but relationship notable)
- **Notes**: Not primarily an ambient location but crucial for Eno's work

## III. Institutions & Schools

### 23. San Francisco Tape Music Center
- **ID**: `loc-sftmc`
- **Type**: Institution
- **Location**: San Francisco, California, USA
- **Significance**: Collective founded by Pauline Oliveros, Morton Subotnick, and Ramon Sender. Commissioned Don Buchla's first synthesizer. Crucial West Coast experimental music incubator.
- **Active Period**: 1962-1966 (moved to Mills College)
- **Connected Persons**: Pauline Oliveros, Don Buchla
- **Notes**: Later became Mills College Center for Contemporary Music

### 24. Mills College
- **ID**: `loc-mills`
- **Type**: University
- **Location**: Oakland, California, USA
- **Significance**: Absorbed San Francisco Tape Music Center. Pauline Oliveros directed Center for Contemporary Music. Major American experimental music education site.
- **Active Period**: 1966-present
- **Connected Persons**: Pauline Oliveros

### 25. Darmstadt Summer Courses
- **ID**: `loc-darmstadt`
- **Type**: Institution (annual courses)
- **Location**: Darmstadt, Germany
- **Significance**: International Summer Courses for New Music, established 1946. Where European and American avant-garde met. La Monte Young encountered Cage's ideas here.
- **Active Period**: 1946-present
- **Connected Persons**: Karlheinz Stockhausen (taught), John Cage (taught), La Monte Young (attended)

### 26. Zodiak Free Arts Lab
- **ID**: `loc-zodiak`
- **Type**: Venue/Collective
- **Location**: Berlin, Germany
- **Significance**: Experimental venue co-founded by Conrad Schnitzler in 1968. Where Tangerine Dream members met. Crucial incubator for Berlin electronic scene.
- **Active Period**: 1968-1969
- **Connected Persons**: Conrad Schnitzler, Edgar Froese, Klaus Schulze
- **Notes**: Short-lived but extremely influential

### 27. Ipswich Art School / Winchester School of Art
- **ID**: `loc-british-art-schools`
- **Type**: Educational institutions
- **Location**: Suffolk and Hampshire, UK
- **Significance**: Art schools where Brian Eno studied (Ipswich, then Winchester). Cybernetics-influenced curriculum shaped his generative music concepts.
- **Active Period**: 1960s
- **Connected Persons**: Brian Eno
- **Notes**: Eno studied under Roy Ascott, learned cybernetics/systems thinking

### 28. Düsseldorf Art Academy
- **ID**: `loc-dusseldorf-academy`
- **Type**: University
- **Location**: Düsseldorf, Germany
- **Significance**: Where Joseph Beuys taught; Conrad Schnitzler studied here. Connection between conceptual art and German experimental music.
- **Active Period**: 1960s
- **Connected Persons**: Conrad Schnitzler, Joseph Beuys (not in enumeration)

## IV. Venues

### 29. The Kitchen
- **ID**: `loc-kitchen`
- **Type**: Performance venue
- **Location**: New York City, USA
- **Significance**: Avant-garde performance space founded 1971. Hosted minimalist and experimental music including Philip Glass, Steve Reich. Important new music venue.
- **Active Period**: 1971-present
- **Connected Persons**: Philip Glass, Steve Reich, La Monte Young

### 30. Washington Square Park
- **ID**: `loc-washington-square`
- **Type**: Public space
- **Location**: New York City, USA
- **Significance**: Where Brian Eno discovered Laraaji busking with electric zither in 1979, leading to *Ambient 3: Day of Radiance*.
- **Date**: 1979
- **Connected Persons**: Brian Eno, Laraaji
- **Notes**: Chance encounter that produced ambient landmark

### 31. Dream House (Installation Site)
- **ID**: `loc-dream-house-install`
- **Type**: Installation venue
- **Location**: New York City (various locations over decades)
- **Significance**: La Monte Young and Marian Zazeela's continuous sound and light environment. Ultimate ambient space—some installations run for years.
- **Active Period**: 1969-present (various incarnations)
- **Connected Persons**: La Monte Young

### 32. La Monte Young's Loft
- **ID**: `loc-young-loft`
- **Type**: Performance/living space
- **Location**: New York City, USA
- **Significance**: Where Theatre of Eternal Music performed. Site of legendary extended drone performances with Tony Conrad, John Cale, etc.
- **Active Period**: 1962-1965
- **Connected Persons**: La Monte Young, Tony Conrad, John Cale (not in enumeration)
