# Protestant Reformation - Locations

**Date**: 2026-01-18
**Last Updated**: 2026-01-18
**Purpose**: Identify significant places for the Protestant Reformation network.

## Location Categories

1. **Cities of Reform**: Urban centers where major reforms occurred
2. **Universities**: Academic institutions central to reform
3. **Churches & Cathedrals**: Sites of preaching and worship innovation
4. **Political Centers**: Courts and seats of power
5. **Printing Centers**: Locations of Protestant publishing
6. **Sites of Debate/Conflict**: Locations of disputations, trials, martyrdoms

## German Cities

### Wittenberg
- **ID**: `location-wittenberg`
- **Title**: Wittenberg
- **Type**: city
- **Country**: Electoral Saxony (modern Germany)
- **Coordinates**: 51.8667° N, 12.6500° E
- **Date Range**: 1502-1560 (peak significance)
- **Significance**: Birthplace of the Lutheran Reformation. Luther's base from 1511 until death (1546). Site of university, Castle Church (95 Theses), and Town Church (St. Mary's).
- **Key Events**:
  - 1517: Luther posts Ninety-Five Theses on Castle Church door
  - 1519: Leipzig Debate origins
  - 1521: Luther returns from Wartburg
  - 1522: Karlstadt's radical reforms; Luther restores order
  - 1525: Luther marries Katharina von Bora
  - 1546: Luther's funeral at Castle Church
- **Key Figures**: Luther, Melanchthon, Bugenhagen, Jonas, Cranach, Katharina von Bora
- **Evidence**: Luther's house, Castle Church, Town Church all survive; UNESCO World Heritage Site

### University of Wittenberg
- **ID**: `location-wittenberg-university`
- **Title**: University of Wittenberg (Leucorea)
- **Type**: university
- **Country**: Electoral Saxony (modern Germany)
- **Parent Location**: location-wittenberg
- **Date Range**: 1502-1817
- **Founded**: 1502 by Frederick the Wise
- **Significance**: Center of Lutheran theological education. Luther and Melanchthon taught here. Trained generation of Lutheran clergy and scholars. "Preceptor of Germany" nickname for Melanchthon's educational influence.
- **Key Events**:
  - 1508: Luther joins faculty
  - 1518: Melanchthon joins as professor of Greek
  - 1520s: Curriculum reformed along humanist and Protestant lines
- **Key Figures**: Luther (theology), Melanchthon (Greek, theology), Bugenhagen (theology), Jonas (law, theology)
- **Evidence**: University records survive; buildings partially preserved

### Castle Church (Wittenberg)
- **ID**: `location-castle-church-wittenberg`
- **Title**: Castle Church (Schlosskirche)
- **Type**: church
- **Country**: Electoral Saxony (modern Germany)
- **Parent Location**: location-wittenberg
- **Significance**: Site where Luther allegedly posted the Ninety-Five Theses (October 31, 1517). Frederick the Wise's relic collection housed here. Luther and Melanchthon buried here.
- **Key Events**:
  - 1517: Ninety-Five Theses posted on door
  - 1546: Luther buried before altar
  - 1560: Melanchthon buried alongside Luther
- **Evidence**: Church survives (rebuilt after 1760 fire); Luther and Melanchthon tombs visible

### Worms
- **ID**: `location-worms`
- **Title**: Worms
- **Type**: city
- **Country**: Holy Roman Empire (modern Germany)
- **Coordinates**: 49.6341° N, 8.3507° E
- **Significance**: Site of the Diet of Worms (1521) where Luther refused to recant before Emperor Charles V. "Here I stand" speech (likely apocryphal but represents his stance).
- **Key Events**:
  - April 17-18, 1521: Luther appears before Diet of Worms
  - April 18, 1521: Luther refuses to recant
  - May 25, 1521: Edict of Worms declares Luther outlaw
- **Key Figures**: Luther, Charles V, Frederick the Wise (protected Luther's departure)
- **Evidence**: Diet records survive; Luther monument in Worms (1868)

### Augsburg
- **ID**: `location-augsburg`
- **Title**: Augsburg
- **Type**: city
- **Country**: Holy Roman Empire (modern Germany)
- **Coordinates**: 48.3705° N, 10.8978° E
- **Significance**: Major imperial city. Site of Luther's 1518 meeting with Cajetan and the 1530 Diet where Augsburg Confession was presented. Wealthy banking city (Fuggers funded indulgences).
- **Key Events**:
  - October 1518: Luther meets Cardinal Cajetan
  - 1530: Diet of Augsburg; Augsburg Confession presented
  - 1555: Peace of Augsburg establishes cuius regio, eius religio
- **Key Figures**: Luther, Melanchthon, Cajetan, Charles V, Fugger banking family
- **Evidence**: St. Anne's Church (Luther's 1518 interview site); Diet records

### Marburg
- **ID**: `location-marburg`
- **Title**: Marburg
- **Type**: city
- **Country**: Landgraviate of Hesse (modern Germany)
- **Coordinates**: 50.8021° N, 8.7670° E
- **Significance**: Site of Marburg Colloquy (1529) where Luther and Zwingli failed to reach agreement on the Eucharist. Protestant university founded 1527.
- **Key Events**:
  - 1527: University of Marburg founded (first Protestant university)
  - October 1-4, 1529: Marburg Colloquy; Luther and Zwingli debate Eucharist
- **Key Figures**: Luther, Zwingli, Melanchthon, Oecolampadius, Bucer, Philip of Hesse (host)
- **Evidence**: Marburg Castle (colloquy site) survives; university records

### University of Marburg
- **ID**: `location-marburg-university`
- **Title**: University of Marburg
- **Type**: university
- **Country**: Landgraviate of Hesse (modern Germany)
- **Parent Location**: location-marburg
- **Date Range**: 1527-present
- **Founded**: 1527 by Philip of Hesse
- **Significance**: First university founded as Protestant institution. Trained Lutheran (later Reformed) clergy. Patrick Hamilton studied here before returning to Scotland.
- **Key Figures**: Philip of Hesse (founder), Patrick Hamilton (student)
- **Evidence**: University survives; oldest Protestant university

### Nuremberg

- **Country**: County of Mansfeld (modern Germany)

### Wartburg Castle
- **ID**: `location-wartburg`
- **Title**: Wartburg Castle
- **Type**: castle
- **Country**: Electoral Saxony (modern Germany)
- **Coordinates**: 50.9667° N, 10.3067° E
- **Significance**: Where Luther was hidden (May 1521 - March 1522) after Diet of Worms under Frederick the Wise's protection. Here Luther translated New Testament into German in 11 weeks.
- **Key Events**:
  - May 1521 - March 1522: Luther in hiding as "Junker Jörg"
  - Winter 1521-22: Luther translates New Testament into German
- **Key Figures**: Luther, Frederick the Wise (arranged protection)
- **Evidence**: Castle survives; Luther's room preserved; UNESCO World Heritage Site

### Erfurt
- **ID**: `location-erfurt`
- **Title**: Erfurt
- **Type**: city
- **Country**: Electorate of Mainz (modern Germany)
- **Coordinates**: 50.9847° N, 11.0299° E
- **Significance**: Luther's university education (1501-1505) and early monastic life. Entered Augustinian monastery here (1505). Important step in Luther's formation.
- **Key Events**:
  - 1501: Luther begins university studies
  - July 2, 1505: Luther's thunderstorm vow; enters monastery
  - 1505-1511: Luther as Augustinian friar
- **Key Figures**: Luther, Johann von Staupitz (mentor)
- **Evidence**: Augustinian monastery (Augustinerkloster) survives; university buildings

### Mühlhausen

- **Type**: city
- **Country**: Thuringia (modern Germany)
- **Coordinates**: 51.2087° N, 10.4528° E

## Swiss Cities

### Zurich
- **ID**: `location-zurich`
- **Title**: Zurich
- **Type**: city
- **Country**: Swiss Confederacy (modern Switzerland)
- **Coordinates**: 47.3769° N, 8.5417° E
- **Significance**: Center of Swiss Reformation under Zwingli. Reformed independently of Luther. Site of disputations that established Reformed Christianity. Later led by Bullinger.
- **Key Events**:
  - 1519: Zwingli begins preaching at Grossmünster
  - January 1523: First Zurich Disputation; city council supports Zwingli
  - 1525: Mass abolished; Zurich fully Reformed
  - 1525: First Anabaptist baptisms; persecution begins
  - October 11, 1531: Zwingli killed at Battle of Kappel
- **Key Figures**: Zwingli, Bullinger, Leo Jud, Grebel, Manz
- **Evidence**: Grossmünster (Zwingli's church) survives; city council records; Zwingli statue

### Grossmünster
- **ID**: `location-grossmunster`
- **Title**: Grossmünster
- **Type**: church
- **Country**: Swiss Confederacy (modern Switzerland)
- **Parent Location**: location-zurich
- **Significance**: Zwingli's church from 1519. Center of Zurich Reformation. Site where Zwingli preached through entire Bible systematically. Later led by Bullinger.
- **Key Events**:
  - 1519: Zwingli begins as people's priest
  - 1520s: Continuous biblical exposition preaching
- **Key Figures**: Zwingli, Bullinger
- **Evidence**: Church survives; Reformation-era modifications visible

### Geneva
- **ID**: `location-geneva`
- **Title**: Geneva
- **Type**: city
- **Country**: Independent city-state / later allied with Swiss Confederacy (modern Switzerland)
- **Coordinates**: 46.2044° N, 6.1432° E
- **Significance**: "Protestant Rome" under Calvin's leadership. Model for Reformed church organization worldwide. Geneva Academy trained Reformed ministers. Marian exiles produced Geneva Bible here.
- **Key Events**:
  - 1536: Geneva adopts Reformation; Farel recruits Calvin
  - 1538: Calvin and Farel expelled
  - 1541: Calvin returns; Ecclesiastical Ordinances established
  - 1553: Servetus executed
  - 1559: Geneva Academy founded
  - 1560: Geneva Bible produced
- **Key Figures**: Calvin, Farel, Beza, Viret, Knox (exile)
- **Evidence**: St. Pierre Cathedral; Reformation Wall monument; Calvin's Geneva survives largely intact

### St. Pierre Cathedral (Geneva)
- **ID**: `location-st-pierre-geneva`
- **Title**: St. Pierre Cathedral
- **Type**: church
- **Country**: Geneva (modern Switzerland)
- **Parent Location**: location-geneva
- **Significance**: Calvin's church from 1541. Center of Genevan Reformed worship. Stripped of Catholic ornamentation. Calvin preached here regularly.
- **Key Features**: Simplified Protestant interior; Calvin's chair preserved
- **Evidence**: Cathedral survives; Calvin's chair displayed

### Geneva Academy
- **ID**: `location-geneva-academy`
- **Title**: Geneva Academy (Académie de Genève)
- **Type**: university
- **Country**: Geneva (modern Switzerland)
- **Parent Location**: location-geneva
- **Date Range**: 1559-present
- **Founded**: 1559 by Calvin; Beza first rector
- **Significance**: Training ground for Reformed ministers from across Europe. Graduates spread Calvinist reformation to France, Netherlands, Scotland, England.
- **Key Figures**: Calvin (founder), Beza (first rector)
- **Evidence**: Later became University of Geneva; records survive

### Basel

- **Type**: city
- **Country**: Swiss Confederacy (modern Switzerland)
- **Coordinates**: 47.5596° N, 7.5886° E

- **Country**: Swiss Confederacy (modern Switzerland)
- **Parent Location**: location-basel
- **Date Range**: 1460-present
- **Significance**: Oldest Swiss university. Where Zwingli and Leo Jud studied under Thomas Wyttenbach. Erasmus associated with it. Center of humanist learning.
- **Key Figures**: Thomas Wyttenbach (taught Zwingli), Erasmus (associated), Zwingli (student), Leo Jud (student)

## Strasbourg

### Strasbourg
- **ID**: `location-strasbourg`
- **Title**: Strasbourg
- **Type**: city
- **Country**: Holy Roman Empire / Imperial Free City (modern France)
- **Coordinates**: 48.5734° N, 7.7521° E
- **Significance**: Important mediating center between Lutheran and Reformed traditions. Bucer and Capito led reform. Calvin lived here (1538-1541) and was shaped by Bucer. Relatively tolerant city that welcomed refugees.
- **Key Events**:
  - 1520s: Reformation established under Bucer and Capito
  - 1530: Tetrapolitan Confession presented
  - 1538-1541: Calvin's exile in Strasbourg
  - 1549: Interim imposed; Bucer leaves for England
- **Key Figures**: Martin Bucer, Wolfgang Capito, Matthew Zell, Katharina Schütz Zell, Calvin (resident)
- **Evidence**: Strasbourg Cathedral (remained Catholic); Temple Neuf; city records

## English Locations

- **Title**: London
- **Type**: city
- **Country**: England
- **Coordinates**: 51.5074° N, 0.1278° E

  - 1540: Cromwell executed; Barnes burned

- **Country**: England

### Cambridge
- **ID**: `location-cambridge`
- **Title**: Cambridge
- **Type**: city
- **Country**: England
- **Coordinates**: 52.2053° N, 0.1218° E
- **Significance**: University city where English Reformation began intellectually. White Horse Tavern meetings (c. 1520s) where reformers gathered. More sympathetic to reform than Oxford.

  - Early 1530s: Cambridge men prominent in reform
- **Key Figures**: Bilney, Barnes, Latimer, Cranmer, Ridley, Coverdale, Parker (all Cambridge men)

- **Type**: university
- **Country**: England

- **Type**: meeting place
- **Country**: England
- **Parent Location**: location-cambridge
- **Date Range**: c. 1520-1530

- **Key Events**:

- **Key Figures**: Bilney, Barnes, Latimer, Coverdale, and others
