## Known Gaps and Limitations

### Geographic Gaps

1. **Portuguese Humanism**: No Portuguese humanists included. Notable figures like Damião de Góis (1502-1574) had connections to Erasmus and should be considered for future expansion.

2. **Eastern European Humanism**: Polish and Hungarian humanism underrepresented.
   - Janus Pannonius (1434-1472) - Hungarian humanist poet
   - Jan Długosz (1415-1480) - Polish historian
   - Could connect to Italian networks through study abroad

3. **Scottish Humanism**: No Scottish figures included. George Buchanan (1506-1582) was significant.

### Thematic Gaps

1. **Women Humanists**: No women included in current enumeration. Notable candidates:
   - Isotta Nogarola (1418-1466) - Veronese humanist
   - Cassandra Fedele (1465-1558) - Venetian scholar
   - Laura Cereta (1469-1499) - Brescian humanist
   - Olimpia Morata (1526-1555) - Ferrara-born scholar
   
   **Rationale for gap**: Documentation of network connections for women humanists is more limited, but they should be considered for future expansion.

2. **Scientific Humanism**: Intersection of humanism and emerging sciences underrepresented.
   - Humanists who contributed to mathematical/scientific texts partially covered (Linacre, Giorgio Valla, Tunstall)
   - Natural philosophy connections could be expanded

3. **Later 16th Century**: Network thins after c.1540. Later figures like Justus Lipsius (1547-1606) could extend the network.

### Source Limitations

1. **Correspondence evidence**: While Erasmus's correspondence is well-documented (3,000+ letters), other figures' correspondence networks are less completely documented.

2. **Teaching relationships**: Some student-teacher relationships inferred from institutional presence rather than direct documentation.

## Contested Claims

### Pico-Ficino Relationship
- **Claim**: Ficino was Pico's "mentor"
- **Status**: Partially contested
- **Detail**: While Ficino was older and influenced Pico, their relationship was more of intellectual peers who sometimes disagreed. Pico critiqued Ficino's positions (e.g., in *De ente et uno*).
- **Resolution**: Documented as "influenced/taught" with note about divergences.

### Donation of Constantine Exposé Impact
- **Claim**: Valla's exposé immediately undermined papal authority
- **Status**: Oversimplified
- **Detail**: Work circulated in manuscript but had limited immediate impact. Greater influence came after Reformation-era publication and polemical use.
- **Resolution**: Noted significance without overclaiming immediate impact.

### Platonic Academy as Institution
- **Claim**: "Academy" was formal institution
- **Status**: Contested by modern scholarship
- **Detail**: The "Florentine Platonic Academy" was an informal network of scholars, not a formal institution with membership, meetings, etc. Term "academy" is conventional but potentially misleading.
- **Resolution**: Described as "informal gathering" in entities file.

### Erasmus's Birth Year
- **Claim**: Born 1466 vs. 1469
- **Status**: Debated
- **Detail**: Two birth years proposed in sources. 1466 more commonly accepted but not certain.
- **Resolution**: Using 1466 as in standard references.

## Recommendations for Future Research

### High Priority Additions

1. **Women humanists**: Add 2-3 women to improve representation. Best candidates:
   - Isotta Nogarola (documented correspondence with male humanists)
   - Cassandra Fedele (known to Poliziano, Ficino's circle)

2. **Portuguese connection**: Add Damião de Góis to connect Iberian and Northern humanism.

3. **Greek transmission**: Consider adding Marcus Musurus (c.1470-1517), Greek scholar who worked with Aldine Press.

### Medium Priority Expansions

1. **Scientific humanism**: Expand coverage of humanists with scientific interests.

2. **Later 16th century**: Extend temporal scope to c.1580 to include:
   - Justus Lipsius (Neo-Stoicism)
   - Joseph Scaliger (chronology, philology)

3. **Second-tier Italian cities**: Add more detail on humanist activity in:
   - Siena
   - Perugia
   - Bologna (beyond university)

1. **Correspondence evidence**: For major figures, add specific letter citations where possible.

2. **Dates**: Verify all approximate dates against recent scholarly sources.

3. **Location specificity**: Add "lived_in" and "worked_at" relationships during conversion.
