## Italian Cities

### Florence
- **ID**: location-florence
- **Type**: city
- **Country**: Italy
- **Coordinates**: 43.7696° N, 11.2558° E
- **Dates of significance**: c.1350-1550
- **Description**: Center of Italian humanism, particularly in the 15th century. Under Medici patronage, Florence became the hub of Renaissance culture, hosting the Platonic Academy and supporting scholars like Ficino, Pico, Poliziano, and Bruni.
- **Associated figures**: Petrarch, Boccaccio, Salutati, Bruni, Poggio, Niccoli, Ficino, Pico, Poliziano, Alberti, Machiavelli, Guicciardini, Lorenzo de' Medici
- **Key institutions**: Platonic Academy, Studio Fiorentino
- **Significance**: Primary center of Italian humanism; civic humanism and Neoplatonism developed here.

### Rome
- **ID**: location-rome
- **Type**: city
- **Country**: Italy (Papal States)
- **Coordinates**: 41.9028° N, 12.4964° E
- **Dates of significance**: c.1420-1550
- **Description**: Seat of the papacy and center of Roman humanism. Papal patronage attracted humanists as secretaries and librarians. Site of antiquarian studies of ancient monuments.
- **Associated figures**: Valla, Biondo, Pomponio Leto, Platina, Pius II, Alberti
- **Key institutions**: Roman Academy (Accademia Romana), Vatican Library, Papal Curia
- **Significance**: Center of papal humanism; antiquarian studies of ancient Rome.

### Venice
- **ID**: location-venice
- **Type**: city
- **Country**: Italy (Venetian Republic)
- **Coordinates**: 45.4408° N, 12.3155° E
- **Dates of significance**: c.1450-1550
- **Description**: Major center of humanist printing through the Aldine Press. Venetian patrician culture supported humanist scholarship. Bessarion donated his library here (Biblioteca Marciana).
- **Associated figures**: Aldus Manutius, Bembo, Ermolao Barbaro, Bessarion, Giorgio Valla, Pietro Aretino
- **Key institutions**: Aldine Press, Biblioteca Marciana
- **Significance**: Center of humanist printing; Venetian patrician humanism.

### Naples
- **ID**: location-naples
- **Type**: city
- **Country**: Italy (Kingdom of Naples)
- **Coordinates**: 40.8518° N, 14.2681° E
- **Dates of significance**: c.1440-1530
- **Description**: Under Aragonese rule, Naples became a significant humanist center. Valla worked under Alfonso V's patronage. Pontano led the Accademia Pontaniana.
- **Associated figures**: Valla, Pontano, Sannazaro, Juan de Valdés
- **Key institutions**: Accademia Pontaniana
- **Significance**: Aragonese court patronage; literary humanism.

### Ferrara
- **ID**: location-ferrara
- **Type**: city
- **Country**: Italy (Este domains)
- **Coordinates**: 44.8381° N, 11.6198° E
- **Dates of significance**: c.1420-1500
- **Description**: Site of famous humanist school run by Guarino da Verona under Este patronage. Center for Greek studies and humanist education.
- **Associated figures**: Guarino da Verona, Battista Guarino, Theodore Gaza
- **Key institutions**: Guarino's school, University of Ferrara
- **Significance**: Model humanist school; Greek émigré teaching.

### Padua
- **ID**: location-padua
- **Type**: city
- **Country**: Italy (Venetian territory)
- **Coordinates**: 45.4064° N, 11.8768° E
- **Dates of significance**: c.1400-1550
- **Description**: University city known for Aristotelian philosophy and humanist teaching. Pietro Pomponazzi and other philosophers taught here.
- **Associated figures**: Pomponazzi, Barzizza, Leonico Tomeo, Vergerio
- **Key institutions**: University of Padua
- **Significance**: Aristotelian philosophy; university humanism.

### Mantua
- **ID**: location-mantua
- **Type**: city
- **Country**: Italy (Gonzaga domains)
- **Coordinates**: 45.1564° N, 10.7914° E
- **Dates of significance**: c.1423-1500
- **Description**: Site of Vittorino da Feltre's famous "Casa Giocosa" school under Gonzaga patronage.
- **Associated figures**: Vittorino da Feltre, Castiglione
- **Key institutions**: Casa Giocosa school
- **Significance**: Model humanist school; court culture.

### Urbino
- **ID**: location-urbino
- **Type**: city
- **Country**: Italy (Montefeltro/della Rovere domains)
- **Coordinates**: 43.7262° N, 12.6366° E
- **Dates of significance**: c.1460-1530
- **Description**: Duke Federico da Montefeltro created a famous humanist court. Setting of Castiglione's *Book of the Courtier*.
- **Associated figures**: Castiglione
- **Key institutions**: Ducal Palace, court of Urbino
- **Significance**: Ideal Renaissance court; setting of *Il Cortegiano*.

### Milan
- **ID**: location-milan
- **Type**: city
- **Country**: Italy (Duchy of Milan)
- **Coordinates**: 45.4642° N, 9.1900° E
- **Dates of significance**: c.1440-1530
- **Description**: Visconti and Sforza courts patronized humanists. Chalcondyles taught here after leaving Florence.
- **Associated figures**: Filelfo, Chalcondyles
- **Key institutions**: Sforza court
- **Significance**: Court humanism; Greek teaching.

## Universities and Academies

### University of Florence (Studio Fiorentino)
- **ID**: location-studio-fiorentino
- **Type**: university
- **Country**: Italy
- **Parent location**: location-florence
- **Dates of significance**: c.1321-1500s
- **Description**: Florentine university where many humanists taught. Chrysoloras, Poliziano, and others lectured here.
- **Associated figures**: Chrysoloras, Poliziano, Argyropoulos, Landino
- **Significance**: Site of Greek teaching and humanist lectures.

### University of Padua
- **ID**: location-university-padua
- **Type**: university
- **Country**: Italy
- **Parent location**: location-padua
- **Dates of significance**: 1222-present
- **Description**: One of Europe's leading universities. Center of Aristotelian philosophy and medical/scientific studies.
- **Associated figures**: Pomponazzi, Barzizza, Leonico Tomeo
- **Significance**: Aristotelian philosophy; university humanism.

### University of Bologna
- **ID**: location-university-bologna
- **Type**: university
- **Country**: Italy
- **Dates of significance**: 1088-present
- **Coordinates**: 44.4949° N, 11.3426° E
- **Description**: Europe's oldest university. Center for legal studies and humanist scholarship.
- **Associated figures**: Pomponazzi (later career)
- **Significance**: Major Italian university.

### University of Alcalá de Henares (Complutense)
- **ID**: location-university-alcala
- **Type**: university
- **Country**: Spain
- **Coordinates**: 40.4820° N, 3.3640° W
- **Dates of significance**: 1508-present (founded by Cisneros)
- **Description**: Founded by Cardinal Cisneros in 1508. Site of the Complutensian Polyglot Bible project.
- **Associated figures**: Cisneros, Nebrija, Hernán Núñez
- **Significance**: Spanish humanist scholarship; trilingual studies.

### University of Paris
- **ID**: location-university-paris
- **Type**: university
- **Country**: France
- **Coordinates**: 48.8499° N, 2.3442° E
- **Dates of significance**: c.1150-present
- **Description**: Major European university. Lefèvre d'Étaples and other French humanists worked here.
- **Associated figures**: Lefèvre d'Étaples, Vives, Postel
- **Significance**: French humanism; theological studies.

### Collège de France
- **ID**: location-college-de-france
- **Type**: academy
- **Country**: France
- **Parent location**: location-paris
- **Dates of significance**: 1530-present
- **Description**: Founded at Budé's urging by Francis I in 1530 as royal lectureships independent of the university. Taught Greek, Hebrew, and mathematics.
- **Associated figures**: Budé, Postel, Dorat
- **Significance**: Humanist alternative to scholastic university.

## Schools

### St. Paul's School (London)
- **ID**: location-st-pauls-school
- **Type**: school
- **Country**: England
- **Parent location**: London
- **Dates of significance**: 1509-present
- **Description**: Model humanist grammar school founded by John Colet (1509). Curriculum designed with Erasmus's input.
- **Associated figures**: Colet, Erasmus
- **Significance**: Model for English humanist education.

### Deventer School
- **ID**: location-deventer-school
- **Type**: school
- **Country**: Netherlands
- **Coordinates**: 52.2557° N, 6.1550° E
- **Dates of significance**: c.1470-1500
- **Description**: Latin school where Alexander Hegius reformed curriculum along humanist lines. Young Erasmus studied here (c.1475-1484).
- **Associated figures**: Hegius, Erasmus, Agricola
- **Significance**: Training ground for Northern humanists.

- **Dates of significance**: 1538-present
- **Description**: Founded by Johannes Sturm (1538). Model for Protestant humanist education across Europe.
- **Associated figures**: Sturm
- **Significance**: Protestant humanist education model.

## Printing Centers

### Venice (Aldine Press)
- **ID**: location-aldine-press
- **Type**: printing house
- **Country**: Italy
- **Parent location**: location-venice
- **Dates of significance**: 1494-1515 (Aldus's lifetime)
- **Description**: Founded by Aldus Manutius (1494). Revolutionized printing with pocket editions (octavos), italic type, and scholarly Greek and Latin editions. Made classical texts widely accessible.
- **Associated figures**: Aldus Manutius, Erasmus, Bembo
- **Significance**: Most important scholarly press of Renaissance.

### Basel (Froben Press)
- **ID**: location-froben-press
- **Type**: printing house
- **Country**: Switzerland
- **Coordinates**: 47.5596° N, 7.5886° E
- **Dates of significance**: c.1491-1527
- **Description**: Johann Froben's press published most of Erasmus's works and made Basel a center of humanist printing north of the Alps.
- **Associated figures**: Froben, Erasmus, Beatus Rhenanus
- **Significance**: Center of Northern humanist publishing.

### Paris (Estienne Press)
- **ID**: location-estienne-press
- **Type**: printing house
- **Country**: France
- **Parent location**: location-paris
- **Dates of significance**: c.1502-1559
- **Description**: Family of scholar-printers. Robert Estienne published critical Bible editions and the *Thesaurus linguae Latinae*.
- **Associated figures**: Robert Estienne
- **Significance**: French scholarly printing.

## Courts and Palaces

### Medici Villa at Careggi
- **ID**: location-villa-careggi
- **Type**: villa
- **Country**: Italy
- **Parent location**: location-florence
- **Dates of significance**: c.1462-1492
- **Description**: Villa outside Florence where Cosimo de' Medici established Ficino and the Platonic Academy met.
- **Associated figures**: Cosimo de' Medici, Lorenzo de' Medici, Ficino, Pico, Poliziano
- **Significance**: Meeting place of Platonic Academy.

### Papal Court (Vatican)
- **ID**: location-vatican
- **Type**: court
- **Country**: Italy (Papal States)
- **Parent location**: location-rome
- **Dates of significance**: c.1400-1550
- **Description**: Papal court employed humanists as secretaries, librarians, and scholars. Popes Nicholas V and Sixtus IV were notable patrons.
- **Associated figures**: Valla, Biondo, Platina, Pius II
- **Key institutions**: Vatican Library (established 1475)
- **Significance**: Papal humanist patronage.

### Court of Francis I (France)
- **ID**: location-court-francis-i
- **Type**: court
- **Country**: France
- **Dates of significance**: 1515-1547
- **Description**: Francis I patronized humanists and founded the Collège de France. His sister Marguerite of Navarre protected reformist humanists.
- **Associated figures**: Budé, Lefèvre d'Étaples, Marguerite of Navarre
- **Significance**: French royal patronage; Collège de France.

### English Court (Henry VIII)
- **ID**: location-english-court
- **Type**: court
- **Country**: England
- **Dates of significance**: 1509-1547
- **Description**: Henry VIII patronized humanists including More and Linacre. More served as Lord Chancellor (1529-1532).
- **Associated figures**: More, Linacre, Vives
- **Significance**: English humanist patronage.

## Northern European Centers

- **Country**: Switzerland
