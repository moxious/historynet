# Renaissance Humanism - Objects (Works)

**Date**: 2026-01-18
**Purpose**: Document key works, manuscripts, and publications in the Renaissance Humanism network.

---

## Foundational Humanist Texts

### Epistolae familiares (Petrarch, c.1345-1366)
- **ID**: object-epistolae-familiares
- **Type**: book (correspondence collection)
- **Creator**: person-francesco-petrarca
- **Date**: c.1345-1366
- **Language**: Latin
- **Description**: Collection of Petrarch's letters modeled on Cicero's correspondence. Established the humanist epistolary tradition and documented Petrarch's intellectual relationships.
- **Significance**: Model for humanist letter-writing; source for humanist networks; Petrarch's discovery of Cicero's letters to Atticus (1345) inspired this collection.

### De viris illustribus (Petrarch, c.1337-1353)
- **ID**: object-de-viris-illustribus
- **Type**: book
- **Creator**: person-francesco-petrarca
- **Date**: c.1337-1353
- **Language**: Latin
- **Description**: Collection of biographies of famous Romans, establishing the humanist genre of biographical writing.
- **Significance**: Influential model for Renaissance biographical writing.

### Decameron (Boccaccio, 1353)
- **ID**: object-decameron
- **Type**: book
- **Creator**: person-giovanni-boccaccio
- **Date**: 1353
- **Language**: Italian
- **Description**: Collection of 100 novellas framed by the Black Death. While vernacular literature, demonstrates humanist themes and became foundational for Italian prose.
- **Significance**: Established Italian prose style; model for European novellas.

### Genealogia deorum gentilium (Boccaccio, c.1360)
- **ID**: object-genealogia-deorum
- **Type**: book (encyclopedic)
- **Creator**: person-giovanni-boccaccio
- **Date**: c.1360
- **Language**: Latin
- **Description**: Encyclopedic work on classical mythology. Used as reference work by humanists and artists for centuries.
- **Significance**: Standard reference for classical mythology throughout Renaissance.

### Elegantiae linguae Latinae (Valla, 1444)
- **ID**: object-elegantiae
- **Type**: book (grammar/rhetoric)
- **Creator**: person-lorenzo-valla
- **Date**: 1444
- **Language**: Latin
- **Description**: Handbook of Latin style establishing standards for classical Latin usage. Became the standard reference for humanist Latin.
- **Significance**: Foundational text for humanist Latin education; printed dozens of times.

### De falso credita et ementita Constantini donatione (Valla, 1440)
- **ID**: object-donation-constantine
- **Type**: treatise
- **Creator**: person-lorenzo-valla
- **Date**: 1440
- **Language**: Latin
- **Description**: Proof through philological analysis that the Donation of Constantine was a medieval forgery. Pioneering application of critical methods to historical documents.
- **Significance**: Foundational text of historical criticism; influenced Erasmus and Protestant reformers.

### Erotemata (Chrysoloras, c.1390s)
- **ID**: object-erotemata
- **Type**: book (grammar)
- **Creator**: person-manuel-chrysoloras
- **Date**: c.1390s
- **Language**: Greek/Latin
- **Description**: Question-and-answer Greek grammar that became the first Greek grammar textbook in Western Europe.
- **Significance**: Standard Greek grammar for first generation of Italian Greek scholars.

## Platonic and Philosophical Works

### Complete Plato Translations (Ficino, 1484)
- **ID**: object-ficino-plato
- **Type**: book (translation)
- **Creator**: person-marsilio-ficino
- **Date**: 1484
- **Language**: Latin (from Greek)
- **Description**: First complete translation of all Plato's dialogues into Latin. Dedicated to Lorenzo de' Medici.
- **Significance**: Made Plato accessible to Western readers; foundation of Renaissance Neoplatonism.

### Theologia Platonica (Ficino, 1482)
- **ID**: object-theologia-platonica
- **Type**: treatise
- **Creator**: person-marsilio-ficino
- **Date**: 1482
- **Language**: Latin
- **Description**: Ficino's major philosophical work arguing for the immortality of the soul and the compatibility of Platonic philosophy with Christianity.
- **Significance**: Key text of Renaissance Neoplatonism; influenced European thought for centuries.

### De amore (Ficino, 1484)
- **ID**: object-de-amore-ficino
- **Type**: treatise (commentary)
- **Creator**: person-marsilio-ficino
- **Date**: 1484
- **Language**: Latin
- **Description**: Commentary on Plato's *Symposium* developing theory of Platonic love.
- **Significance**: Foundational text for Renaissance love theory; influenced poetry and art.

### Corpus Hermeticum Translation (Ficino, 1463)
- **ID**: object-corpus-hermeticum
- **Type**: book (translation)
- **Creator**: person-marsilio-ficino
- **Date**: 1463
- **Language**: Latin (from Greek)
- **Description**: Translation of the Hermetic texts attributed to Hermes Trismegistus. Cosimo de' Medici requested this before Plato.
- **Significance**: Major influence on Renaissance magic, philosophy, and esoteric thought.

### Oratio de hominis dignitate (Pico, 1486)
- **ID**: object-oration-dignity-man
- **Type**: treatise (oration)
- **Creator**: person-giovanni-pico
- **Date**: 1486
- **Language**: Latin
- **Description**: Often called the "Manifesto of the Renaissance." Celebrates human potential and freedom to shape one's own nature.
- **Significance**: Most famous statement of Renaissance humanism's view of human dignity.

### 900 Theses (Pico, 1486)
- **ID**: object-900-theses
- **Type**: treatise
- **Creator**: person-giovanni-pico
- **Date**: 1486
- **Language**: Latin
- **Description**: 900 propositions from diverse philosophical and religious traditions that Pico proposed to defend in Rome. 13 were condemned by Pope Innocent VIII.
- **Significance**: Ambitious attempt at philosophical synthesis; controversial for engaging Kabbalah and non-Christian sources.

### De ente et uno (Pico, 1491)
- **ID**: object-de-ente-et-uno
- **Type**: treatise
- **Creator**: person-giovanni-pico
- **Date**: 1491
- **Language**: Latin
- **Description**: Philosophical treatise arguing against Ficino's distinction between the One and Being. Attempt to reconcile Plato and Aristotle.
- **Significance**: Shows philosophical debates within Platonic Academy.

### Plotinus Translation (Ficino, 1492)
- **ID**: object-ficino-plotinus
- **Type**: book (translation)
- **Creator**: person-marsilio-ficino
- **Date**: 1492
- **Language**: Latin (from Greek)
- **Description**: First Latin translation of Plotinus's Enneads.
- **Significance**: Made Neoplatonic source texts available to Western readers.

## Political and Civic Writings

### Laudatio Florentinae urbis (Bruni, c.1403-4)
- **ID**: object-laudatio-florentinae
- **Type**: treatise (panegyric)
- **Creator**: person-leonardo-bruni
- **Date**: c.1403-1404
- **Language**: Latin
- **Description**: Praise of Florence modeled on Aelius Aristides's panegyric of Athens. Celebrates Florentine republican liberty and culture.
- **Significance**: Key text of civic humanism linking classical republicanism to Florence.

### Historiarum Florentini populi libri XII (Bruni, 1416-1442)
- **ID**: object-bruni-history-florence
- **Type**: book (history)
- **Creator**: person-leonardo-bruni
- **Date**: 1416-1442
- **Language**: Latin
- **Description**: History of Florence in 12 books, applying humanist historical methods.
- **Significance**: Model for humanist historiography.

### Il Principe (Machiavelli, 1513/1532)
- **ID**: object-il-principe
- **Type**: treatise
- **Creator**: person-niccolo-machiavelli
- **Date**: written 1513, published 1532
- **Language**: Italian
- **Description**: Treatise on political power and statecraft. Analyzes how rulers acquire and maintain power through realistic rather than moralistic lens.
- **Significance**: Foundational text of modern political philosophy; enduringly controversial.

### Discorsi sopra la prima deca di Tito Livio (Machiavelli, c.1517/1531)
- **ID**: object-discorsi-livy
- **Type**: treatise
- **Creator**: person-niccolo-machiavelli
- **Date**: written c.1517, published 1531
- **Language**: Italian
- **Description**: Commentary on Livy's history exploring republican government, civic virtue, and political change.
- **Significance**: Major work of republican political theory; complements *The Prince*.

### Istorie fiorentine (Machiavelli, 1525)
- **ID**: object-istorie-fiorentine
- **Type**: book (history)
- **Creator**: person-niccolo-machiavelli
- **Date**: 1525
- **Language**: Italian
- **Description**: History of Florence commissioned by Medici, applying Machiavelli's political analysis to Florentine history.
- **Significance**: Humanist history with political analysis.

### Storia d'Italia (Guicciardini, 1537-1540/1561)
- **ID**: object-storia-italia
- **Type**: book (history)
- **Creator**: person-francesco-guicciardini
- **Date**: written 1537-1540, published 1561
- **Language**: Italian
- **Description**: History of Italy from 1494 to 1534. Pioneer of critical historical method using archival sources and psychological analysis.
- **Significance**: Greatest Renaissance historical work; model of modern historiography.

### Ricordi (Guicciardini, c.1530)
- **ID**: object-ricordi
- **Type**: treatise (maxims)
- **Creator**: person-francesco-guicciardini
- **Date**: c.1530
- **Language**: Italian
- **Description**: Collection of political and personal maxims reflecting on experience and statecraft.
- **Significance**: Classic of Italian political thought; read alongside Machiavelli.

### Il libro del cortegiano (Castiglione, 1528)
- **ID**: object-book-courtier
- **Type**: treatise (dialogue)
- **Creator**: person-baldassare-castiglione
- **Date**: 1528
- **Language**: Italian
- **Description**: Dialogue set at the court of Urbino defining the ideal courtier: accomplishments, behavior, speech, and *sprezzatura* (studied nonchalance).
- **Significance**: Definitive guide to Renaissance court culture; translated across Europe.

### Della vita civile (Palmieri, c.1435)
- **ID**: object-della-vita-civile
- **Type**: treatise (dialogue)
- **Creator**: person-matteo-palmieri
- **Date**: c.1435
- **Language**: Italian
- **Description**: Dialogue on civic virtue and education in the tradition of Bruni's civic humanism.
- **Significance**: Example of civic humanist tradition.

### De dignitate et excellentia hominis (Manetti, 1452-53)
- **ID**: object-de-dignitate-manetti
- **Type**: treatise
- **Creator**: person-giannozzo-manetti
- **Date**: 1452-1453
- **Language**: Latin
- **Description**: Celebration of human capabilities and dignity, preceding Pico's more famous *Oration*.
- **Significance**: Early statement of humanist view of human dignity.

## Educational Treatises

### De ingenuis moribus et liberalibus adulescentiae studiis (Vergerio, c.1402)
- **ID**: object-de-ingenuis-moribus
- **Type**: treatise
- **Creator**: person-pier-paolo-vergerio
- **Date**: c.1402
- **Language**: Latin
- **Description**: First humanist treatise on education, outlining the liberal arts curriculum that became standard for humanist schools.
- **Significance**: Foundational educational treatise of Renaissance humanism.

### De ordine docendi et studendi (Battista Guarino, 1459)
- **ID**: object-de-ordine-docendi
- **Type**: treatise
- **Creator**: person-battista-guarino
- **Date**: 1459
- **Language**: Latin
- **Description**: Guide to humanist education based on practices of Guarino da Verona's Ferrara school.
- **Significance**: Influential guide to humanist educational methods.

### De inventione dialectica (Agricola, 1479)
- **ID**: object-de-inventione-dialectica
- **Type**: treatise
- **Creator**: person-rudolf-agricola
- **Date**: 1479 (published posthumously 1515)
- **Language**: Latin
- **Description**: Reformed logic emphasizing rhetoric over scholastic dialectic. Major influence on Northern European humanist education.
- **Significance**: Transformed logic teaching; influenced Erasmus and Protestant education.

### De tradendis disciplinis (Vives, 1531)
- **ID**: object-de-tradendis
- **Type**: treatise
- **Creator**: person-juan-luis-vives
- **Date**: 1531
- **Language**: Latin
- **Description**: Comprehensive educational treatise covering curriculum, teaching methods, and philosophy of education.
- **Significance**: Major humanist educational treatise of Northern Renaissance.

### De copia (Erasmus, 1512)
- **ID**: object-de-copia
- **Type**: treatise (rhetoric)
- **Creator**: person-desiderius-erasmus
- **Date**: 1512
- **Language**: Latin
- **Description**: Textbook on verbal abundance and rhetorical variation. Taught through endless variations on themes.
- **Significance**: Standard humanist rhetoric textbook; widely used for centuries.

## Biblical and Religious Scholarship

### Novum Instrumentum (Erasmus, 1516)
- **ID**: object-novum-instrumentum
- **Type**: book (biblical edition)
- **Creator**: person-desiderius-erasmus
- **Date**: 1516
- **Language**: Greek and Latin
- **Description**: First published Greek New Testament with new Latin translation. Based on manuscript comparison. Revised in subsequent editions as *Novum Testamentum*.
- **Significance**: Foundation of modern biblical scholarship; used by Protestant reformers.

### Annotations on the New Testament (Valla, c.1449/1505)
- **ID**: object-valla-annotations-nt
- **Type**: treatise
- **Creator**: person-lorenzo-valla
- **Date**: written c.1449, published by Erasmus 1505
- **Language**: Latin
- **Description**: Philological notes comparing Greek New Testament to Vulgate Latin translation. Discovered and published by Erasmus.
- **Significance**: Applied humanist textual criticism to Scripture; influenced Erasmus.

### Enchiridion militis Christiani (Erasmus, 1503)
- **ID**: object-enchiridion
- **Type**: treatise
- **Creator**: person-desiderius-erasmus
- **Date**: 1503
- **Language**: Latin
- **Description**: Handbook of the Christian soldier, advocating inner piety over ritual observance. Influential statement of humanist Christianity.
- **Significance**: Key text of Christian humanism; widely translated.

### Complutensian Polyglot Bible (ed. Cisneros team, 1514-1517)
- **ID**: object-complutensian-polyglot
- **Type**: book (biblical edition)
- **Creator**: person-cardinal-cisneros (patron), with person-antonio-nebrija, person-hernan-nunez
- **Date**: printed 1514-1517, distributed 1522
- **Language**: Hebrew, Greek, Latin, Aramaic
- **Description**: First printed polyglot Bible with Hebrew, Greek, and Latin in parallel columns. Produced at University of Alcalá under Cardinal Cisneros.
- **Significance**: Major scholarly achievement; though printed before Erasmus's NT, distributed later.

### French Bible Translation (Lefèvre d'Étaples, 1530)
- **ID**: object-french-bible-lefevre
- **Type**: book (translation)
- **Creator**: person-jacques-lefevre-detaples
- **Date**: 1530
- **Language**: French
- **Description**: First complete French translation of the Bible from Vulgate. Anticipated Protestant vernacular Bibles.
- **Significance**: Major vernacular Bible; part of French evangelical humanism.

### De rudimentis Hebraicis (Reuchlin, 1506)
- **ID**: object-de-rudimentis-hebraicis
- **Type**: book (grammar)
- **Creator**: person-johannes-reuchlin
- **Date**: 1506
- **Language**: Latin/Hebrew
- **Description**: First Hebrew grammar by a Christian scholar. Combined grammar, lexicon, and biblical commentary.
- **Significance**: Foundation of Christian Hebraism; enabled Christian study of Hebrew.

### De arte cabalistica (Reuchlin, 1517)
- **ID**: object-de-arte-cabalistica
- **Type**: treatise
- **Creator**: person-johannes-reuchlin
- **Date**: 1517
- **Language**: Latin
- **Description**: Treatise on Kabbalah in dialogue form. Argued for Christian use of Jewish mystical traditions.
- **Significance**: Major text of Christian Kabbalah; controversial but influential.

## Literary Works

### Moriae encomium (Praise of Folly) (Erasmus, 1509)
- **ID**: object-praise-of-folly
- **Type**: book (satire)
- **Creator**: person-desiderius-erasmus
- **Date**: 1509
- **Language**: Latin
- **Description**: Satirical declamation in which Folly praises herself. Written during visit to Thomas More (punning on *moria*/More). Satirizes human vanity, especially among clergy and scholars.
- **Significance**: Most popular of Erasmus's works; witty critique of contemporary society.

### Colloquia familiaria (Erasmus, 1518 onwards)
- **ID**: object-colloquia
- **Type**: book (dialogues)
- **Creator**: person-desiderius-erasmus
- **Date**: 1518 (expanded in later editions)
- **Language**: Latin
- **Description**: Collection of dialogues covering education, religion, and daily life. Used as Latin textbook but also vehicle for reform ideas.
- **Significance**: Widely read Latin textbook; spread Erasmian ideas.

### Utopia (More, 1516)
- **ID**: object-utopia
- **Type**: book
- **Creator**: person-thomas-more
- **Date**: 1516
- **Language**: Latin
- **Description**: Description of ideal island society with communal property and religious toleration. Invented the genre of utopian literature.
- **Significance**: Foundational text of political philosophy and utopian literature.

### Arcadia (Sannazaro, 1504)
- **ID**: object-arcadia
- **Type**: book (pastoral romance)
- **Creator**: person-jacopo-sannazaro
- **Date**: 1504
- **Language**: Italian
- **Description**: Pastoral romance mixing prose and verse. Set an idealized pastoral world.
- **Significance**: Influential on European pastoral literature for two centuries.

### Stanze per la giostra (Poliziano, 1478)
- **ID**: object-stanze-giostra
- **Type**: poem
- **Creator**: person-angelo-poliziano
- **Date**: 1478
- **Language**: Italian
- **Description**: Unfinished poem celebrating a joust by Giuliano de' Medici. Mythological allegory influenced by Neoplatonism.
- **Significance**: Major Italian Renaissance poetry; shows humanist-vernacular synthesis.

### Miscellanea (Poliziano, 1489)
- **ID**: object-miscellanea
- **Type**: book (philology)
- **Creator**: person-angelo-poliziano
- **Date**: 1489
- **Language**: Latin
- **Description**: Collection of 100 philological essays solving textual problems in classical authors. Developed critical methods.
- **Significance**: Foundational text of modern philology.

### Essais (Montaigne, 1580-1592)
- **ID**: object-essais
- **Type**: book (essays)
- **Creator**: person-michel-de-montaigne
- **Date**: 1580, expanded 1588, 1592
- **Language**: French
- **Description**: Invented the essay form. Introspective exploration of human nature drawing on classical sources while examining personal experience.
- **Significance**: Created the essay genre; enormously influential on European thought.

### Discours de la servitude volontaire (La Boétie, c.1549)
- **ID**: object-servitude-volontaire
- **Type**: treatise
- **Creator**: person-etienne-de-la-boetie
- **Date**: c.1549 (circulated in manuscript; published 1576)
- **Language**: French
- **Description**: Early critique of tyranny and analysis of why people submit to oppression.
- **Significance**: Influential on political thought; friend of Montaigne.

### Adagiorum chiliades (Adages) (Erasmus, 1508)
- **ID**: object-adages
- **Type**: book (reference)
- **Creator**: person-desiderius-erasmus
- **Date**: 1508 (greatly expanded from 1500 edition)
- **Language**: Latin
- **Description**: Collection of over 3,000 classical proverbs with commentary. Published by Aldine Press. Expanded throughout Erasmus's life.
- **Significance**: Hugely popular reference work; spread classical wisdom.

## Architectural and Artistic Theory

### De pictura (Alberti, 1435)
- **ID**: object-de-pictura
- **Type**: treatise
- **Creator**: person-leon-battista-alberti
- **Date**: 1435 (Latin), 1436 (Italian as *Della pittura*)
- **Language**: Latin/Italian
- **Description**: First modern treatise on painting. Explains perspective, composition, and the intellectual basis of art. Dedicated to Brunelleschi.
- **Significance**: Foundational text for Renaissance art theory.

### De re aedificatoria (Alberti, c.1452)
- **ID**: object-de-re-aedificatoria
- **Type**: treatise
- **Creator**: person-leon-battista-alberti
- **Date**: c.1452 (published 1485)
- **Language**: Latin
- **Description**: Treatise on architecture modeled on Vitruvius. First modern architectural treatise covering design, construction, and urban planning.
- **Significance**: Major influence on Renaissance architecture.

### De statua (Alberti, c.1464)
- **ID**: object-de-statua
- **Type**: treatise
- **Creator**: person-leon-battista-alberti
- **Date**: c.1464
- **Language**: Latin
- **Description**: Treatise on sculpture, including system for proportions.
- **Significance**: Part of Alberti's comprehensive art theory.

### Della famiglia (Alberti, c.1433-1441)
- **ID**: object-della-famiglia
- **Type**: dialogue
- **Creator**: person-leon-battista-alberti
- **Date**: c.1433-1441
