# Enlightenment Intellectual Network - Key Objects

**Date**: 2026-01-18
**Purpose**: Identify key works, publications, and manuscripts for the Enlightenment network.

---

## Overview

This document catalogs the major intellectual works that shaped and defined the Enlightenment. These objects serve multiple functions in the network:
- **Evidence** for relationships (e.g., responses, critiques, translations)
- **Connection points** linking authors to movements and ideas
- **Historical anchors** providing temporal structure to the network

---

## 1. Foundational Works (Pre-1720)

### Philosophiæ Naturalis Principia Mathematica (1687)
- **ID**: `object-principia-mathematica`
- **Type**: Book (natural philosophy)
- **Creator**: Isaac Newton
- **Date**: 1687 (3rd edition 1726)
- **Language**: Latin
- **Significance**: Established mathematical physics and the mechanistic worldview. Enlightenment thinkers sought to extend Newton's method to human affairs. Symbol of reason's triumph over scholasticism.
- **Network Role**: Influenced virtually all Enlightenment natural philosophy; translated by Émilie du Châtelet into French (1759).
- **External Links**: [Newton Project](http://www.newtonproject.ox.ac.uk/)

### Essay Concerning Human Understanding (1689)
- **ID**: `object-essay-human-understanding`
- **Type**: Book (philosophy)
- **Creator**: John Locke
- **Date**: 1689
- **Language**: English
- **Significance**: Foundational text of empiricism. Argued all knowledge derives from experience, rejecting innate ideas. Shaped Enlightenment epistemology across Europe.
- **Network Role**: Influenced Voltaire, Hume, Condillac, Berkeley; foundation for entire empiricist tradition.

### Two Treatises of Government (1689)
- **ID**: `object-two-treatises`
- **Type**: Book (political philosophy)
- **Creator**: John Locke
- **Date**: 1689
- **Language**: English
- **Significance**: Established theory of natural rights, social contract, and legitimate revolution. Foundation of liberal political thought.
- **Network Role**: Influenced Montesquieu, Rousseau, American founders (Jefferson, Madison).

### A Letter Concerning Toleration (1689)
- **ID**: `object-letter-toleration`
- **Type**: Letter/treatise (political philosophy)
- **Creator**: John Locke
- **Date**: 1689
- **Language**: Latin (English translation 1689)
- **Significance**: Classic argument for religious toleration and separation of church and state.
- **Network Role**: Influenced Voltaire's campaigns for toleration.

### Opticks (1704)
- **ID**: `object-opticks`
- **Type**: Book (natural philosophy)
- **Creator**: Isaac Newton
- **Date**: 1704
- **Language**: English
- **Significance**: Established corpuscular theory of light; more accessible than *Principia*. Widely read by philosophes.
- **Network Role**: Voltaire popularized Newton's optics in France.

### Theodicy (1710)
- **ID**: `object-theodicy`
- **Type**: Book (philosophy/theology)
- **Creator**: Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz
- **Date**: 1710
- **Language**: French
- **Significance**: Argued this is the "best of all possible worlds." Major target of Enlightenment critique.
- **Network Role**: Satirized by Voltaire in *Candide*; influenced Wolff's systematic philosophy.

### Characteristics of Men, Manners, Opinions, Times (1711)
- **ID**: `object-characteristics`
- **Type**: Book (moral philosophy)
- **Creator**: 3rd Earl of Shaftesbury
- **Date**: 1711
- **Language**: English
- **Significance**: Developed moral sense theory—humans have innate capacity for moral judgment. Influential alternative to Lockean tabula rasa.
- **Network Role**: Foundation for Hutcheson's moral philosophy and through him the Scottish school.

### The Fable of the Bees (1714)
- **ID**: `object-fable-bees`
- **Type**: Book (satire/philosophy)
- **Creator**: Bernard Mandeville
- **Date**: 1714 (expanded 1723)
- **Language**: English
- **Significance**: Scandalous argument that private vices produce public benefits. Challenged traditional virtue ethics.
- **Network Role**: Provoked Hutcheson; influenced Smith's economic thought; debated throughout the century.

### Monadology (1714)
- **ID**: `object-monadology`
- **Type**: Treatise (metaphysics)
- **Creator**: Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz
- **Date**: 1714 (published 1720)
- **Language**: French
- **Significance**: Presented Leibniz's metaphysical system of monads—simple substances constituting reality.
- **Network Role**: Systematized by Wolff; critiqued by Kant.

## 2. French Enlightenment Works (1720-1780)

### Persian Letters (1721)
- **ID**: `object-persian-letters`
- **Type**: Epistolary novel
- **Creator**: Montesquieu
- **Date**: 1721
- **Language**: French
- **Significance**: Satirized French society through fictional Persian visitors. Pioneering work of Enlightenment social criticism.
- **Network Role**: Established Montesquieu's reputation; model for comparative cultural analysis.

### Letters on the English (Lettres philosophiques) (1733)
- **ID**: `object-letters-english`
- **Type**: Book (essays)
- **Creator**: Voltaire
- **Date**: 1733 (French edition 1734)
- **Language**: French/English
- **Significance**: Praised English institutions, science (Newton), and philosophy (Locke) as implicit criticism of France. Called "the first bomb hurled against the Ancien Régime."
- **Network Role**: Introduced Locke and Newton to French readers; caused Voltaire's exile.

### Elements of the Philosophy of Newton (1738)
- **ID**: `object-elements-newton`
- **Type**: Book (popular science)
- **Creator**: Voltaire (with Émilie du Châtelet)
- **Date**: 1738
- **Language**: French
- **Significance**: Made Newtonian physics accessible to French general readers. Key work in spreading Newton's ideas on the Continent.
- **Network Role**: Product of Voltaire-du Châtelet collaboration at Cirey.

### Traité de dynamique (1743)
- **ID**: `object-traite-dynamique`
- **Type**: Book (mathematical physics)
- **Creator**: Jean le Rond d'Alembert
- **Date**: 1743
- **Language**: French
- **Significance**: Major contribution to mechanics. Introduced d'Alembert's principle. Established his mathematical reputation.
- **Network Role**: Foundation for d'Alembert's role in *Encyclopédie*.

### Essay on the Origin of Human Knowledge (1746)
- **ID**: `object-essay-origin-knowledge`
- **Type**: Book (epistemology)
- **Creator**: Étienne Bonnot de Condillac
- **Date**: 1746
- **Language**: French
- **Significance**: Extended Locke's empiricism, arguing all mental operations derive from sensation.
- **Network Role**: Foundation of French sensationalist psychology.

### Natural History of the Soul (1745)
- **ID**: `object-natural-history-soul`
- **Type**: Book (materialist philosophy)
- **Creator**: Julien Offray de La Mettrie
- **Date**: 1745
- **Language**: French
- **Significance**: Early statement of La Mettrie's materialism. Caused controversy and his exile.
- **Network Role**: Precursor to *L'Homme Machine*.

### Man a Machine (L'Homme Machine) (1747)
- **ID**: `object-homme-machine`
- **Type**: Book (materialist philosophy)
- **Creator**: Julien Offray de La Mettrie
- **Date**: 1747
- **Language**: French
- **Significance**: Radical materialism arguing humans are sophisticated machines. Pushed Enlightenment thought to controversial extremes.
- **Network Role**: Influenced d'Holbach's materialism; too radical for many philosophes.

### The Spirit of the Laws (De l'esprit des lois) (1748)
- **ID**: `object-spirit-laws`
- **Type**: Book (political philosophy)
- **Creator**: Montesquieu
- **Date**: 1748
- **Language**: French
- **Significance**: Monumental work of comparative political analysis. Articulated separation of powers doctrine. One of the most influential political works of the century.
- **Network Role**: Influenced American Constitution (Madison, Hamilton); placed on Catholic Index (1751).
- **External Links**: [Liberty Fund edition](https://oll.libertyfund.org/title/montesquieu-complete-works-vol-1-the-spirit-of-laws)

### Histoire naturelle (1749-1804)
- **ID**: `object-histoire-naturelle`
- **Type**: Book series (natural history)
- **Creator**: Georges-Louis Leclerc, Comte de Buffon
- **Date**: 1749-1804 (44 volumes)
- **Language**: French
- **Significance**: Most widely read scientific work of the 18th century. Speculated on earth's age (challenging biblical chronology) and species change.
- **Network Role**: Model of comprehensive natural science; published from Jardin du Roi.

### Encyclopédie (1751-1772)
- **ID**: `object-encyclopedie`
- **Type**: Reference work (encyclopedia)
- **Creators**: Denis Diderot (editor), Jean le Rond d'Alembert (co-editor)
- **Date**: 1751-1772 (17 volumes of text, 11 of plates)
- **Language**: French
- **Significance**: THE defining project of the French Enlightenment. Over 70,000 articles covering arts, sciences, trades. Major contributors: Jaucourt (~17,288 articles), Voltaire (~45), Rousseau (music), d'Holbach (chemistry), Quesnay (economics), Turgot.
- **Network Role**: Central node connecting most French philosophes. Supported by Madame Geoffrin.
- **External Links**: [ARTFL Encyclopédie Project](https://encyclopedie.uchicago.edu/)

### Preliminary Discourse to the Encyclopedia (1751)
- **ID**: `object-preliminary-discourse`
- **Type**: Essay (philosophy of knowledge)
- **Creator**: Jean le Rond d'Alembert
- **Date**: 1751
- **Language**: French
- **Significance**: Famous introduction to the *Encyclopédie*. Mapped human knowledge following Bacon; articulated Enlightenment epistemology.
- **Network Role**: D'Alembert's most famous philosophical text.

### Treatise on Sensations (1754)
- **ID**: `object-treatise-sensations`
- **Type**: Book (epistemology)
- **Creator**: Étienne Bonnot de Condillac
- **Date**: 1754
- **Language**: French
- **Significance**: Famous thought experiment of a statue gradually acquiring senses. Systematic development of sensationalism.
- **Network Role**: Mature statement of French empiricist psychology.

### Discourse on the Origin of Inequality (1755)
- **ID**: `object-discourse-inequality`
- **Type**: Book (political philosophy)
- **Creator**: Jean-Jacques Rousseau
- **Date**: 1755
- **Language**: French
- **Significance**: Argued civilization corrupted natural human goodness. Radical critique of progress and property.
- **Network Role**: Provoked Voltaire's mockery; shaped debates on nature vs. civilization.

### De l'esprit (On Mind) (1758)
- **ID**: `object-de-lesprit`
- **Type**: Book (moral philosophy)
- **Creator**: Claude-Adrien Helvétius
- **Date**: 1758
- **Language**: French
- **Significance**: Utilitarian ethics reducing virtue to self-interest properly channeled. Condemned by Sorbonne and ordered burned.
- **Network Role**: Influenced Bentham's utilitarianism; scandal highlighted Enlightenment's radical edge.

### Tableau économique (1758)
- **ID**: `object-tableau-economique`
- **Type**: Diagram/treatise (economics)
- **Creator**: François Quesnay
- **Date**: 1758
- **Language**: French
- **Significance**: First attempt to model economic flows mathematically. Foundation of Physiocracy—land as sole source of wealth.
- **Network Role**: Influenced Smith (met Quesnay in Paris); foundation of economic science.

### Candide (1759)
- **ID**: `object-candide`
- **Type**: Novella (satire)
- **Creator**: Voltaire
- **Date**: 1759
- **Language**: French
- **Significance**: Brilliant satire of Leibnizian optimism ("best of all possible worlds"). Response to Lisbon earthquake (1755). Most famous work of Enlightenment literature.
- **Network Role**: Attacked Leibniz's theodicy; crystallized Enlightenment rejection of metaphysical optimism.

### Christianity Unveiled (1761)
- **ID**: `object-christianity-unveiled`
- **Type**: Book (atheist polemic)
- **Creator**: Baron d'Holbach (anonymous)
- **Date**: 1761
- **Language**: French
- **Significance**: Systematic attack on Christianity. Published anonymously due to danger.
- **Network Role**: Product of d'Holbach salon's radical circle.

### The Social Contract (Du contrat social) (1762)
- **ID**: `object-social-contract`
- **Type**: Book (political philosophy)
- **Creator**: Jean-Jacques Rousseau
- **Date**: 1762
- **Language**: French
- **Significance**: Theory of legitimate political authority based on "general will." Influenced French Revolution and democratic theory.
- **Network Role**: Major work of Enlightenment political thought, though debated by other philosophes.

### Emile, or On Education (1762)
- **ID**: `object-emile`
- **Type**: Book (educational philosophy)
- **Creator**: Jean-Jacques Rousseau
- **Date**: 1762
- **Language**: French
- **Significance**: Revolutionary educational philosophy emphasizing natural development. Influenced Kant's ethics and modern pedagogy.
- **Network Role**: Banned in Paris and Geneva; forced Rousseau's flight.

### Philosophical Dictionary (1764)
- **ID**: `object-philosophical-dictionary`
- **Type**: Book (reference/polemic)
- **Creator**: Voltaire
- **Date**: 1764
- **Language**: French
- **Significance**: Alphabetically arranged philosophical provocations. Portable, accessible, witty attacks on religious intolerance and superstition.
- **Network Role**: Voltaire's most concentrated statement of his views.

### Reflections on the Formation and Distribution of Wealth (1766)
- **ID**: `object-reflections-wealth`
- **Type**: Treatise (economics)
- **Creator**: Anne-Robert-Jacques Turgot
- **Date**: 1766
- **Language**: French
- **Significance**: Major economic treatise going beyond strict Physiocracy. Analyzed capital, wages, and economic development.
- **Network Role**: Influenced Adam Smith; product of Physiocrat circle.

### The System of Nature (1770)
- **ID**: `object-system-nature`
- **Type**: Book (materialist philosophy)
- **Creator**: Baron d'Holbach (anonymous)
- **Date**: 1770
- **Language**: French
- **Significance**: Comprehensive atheist and materialist philosophy. Argued universe operates by necessary physical laws with no divine intervention.
- **Network Role**: Most systematic statement of radical Enlightenment materialism; debated throughout Europe.

### Sketch for a Historical Picture of the Progress of the Human Mind (1795)
- **ID**: `object-sketch-progress`
- **Type**: Book (philosophy of history)
- **Creator**: Nicolas de Condorcet
- **Date**: 1795 (written 1793-94, published posthumously)
- **Language**: French
- **Significance**: Classic statement of Enlightenment faith in progress. Written while hiding from Revolutionary authorities.
- **Network Role**: Definitive summary of Enlightenment optimism about human improvement.

## 3. British and Scottish Works (1710-1790)

### A Treatise Concerning the Principles of Human Knowledge (1710)
- **ID**: `object-principles-human-knowledge`
- **Type**: Book (philosophy)
- **Creator**: George Berkeley
- **Date**: 1710
- **Language**: English
- **Significance**: Developed idealist empiricism—"to be is to be perceived." Denied existence of matter independent of perception.
- **Network Role**: Built on and critiqued Locke; influenced Hume's skepticism.

### Three Dialogues between Hylas and Philonous (1713)
- **ID**: `object-three-dialogues`
- **Type**: Book (philosophy)
- **Creator**: George Berkeley
- **Date**: 1713
- **Language**: English
- **Significance**: Accessible dialogue form defense of Berkeley's immaterialism.
- **Network Role**: Companion to *Principles*; engaged wider audience.

### Inquiry into the Original of Our Ideas of Beauty and Virtue (1725)
- **ID**: `object-inquiry-beauty-virtue`
- **Type**: Book (moral philosophy)
- **Creator**: Francis Hutcheson
- **Date**: 1725
- **Language**: English
- **Significance**: Systematic development of moral sense theory. Foundation of Scottish Enlightenment ethics.
- **Network Role**: Influenced Hume and Adam Smith; response to Mandeville.

### A Treatise of Human Nature (1739-40)
- **ID**: `object-treatise-human-nature`
- **Type**: Book (philosophy)
- **Creator**: David Hume
- **Date**: 1739-40
- **Language**: English
- **Significance**: Hume's masterpiece, though it "fell dead-born from the press." Radical empiricist analysis of causation, self, and morals.
- **Network Role**: Foundation of Hume's philosophy; later reworked as *Enquiries*.
- **External Links**: [David Hume Texts](https://davidhume.org/)

### An Enquiry Concerning Human Understanding (1748)
- **ID**: `object-enquiry-human-understanding`
- **Type**: Book (philosophy)
- **Creator**: David Hume
- **Date**: 1748
- **Language**: English
- **Significance**: Reworked version of *Treatise* Book I. Famous analysis of causation and miracles. More successful than *Treatise*.
- **Network Role**: "Awakened Kant from dogmatic slumber"; widely read in Europe.

### Essays on the Principles of Morality and Natural Religion (1751)
- **ID**: `object-essays-morality-religion`
- **Type**: Book (philosophy)
- **Creator**: Henry Home, Lord Kames
- **Date**: 1751
- **Language**: English
- **Significance**: Explored moral sense and natural religion. Controversial for apparent necessity of human actions.
- **Network Role**: Major work by Scottish Enlightenment patron.

### History of England (1754-61)
- **ID**: `object-history-england`
- **Type**: Book (history)
- **Creator**: David Hume
- **Date**: 1754-1761 (6 volumes)
- **Language**: English
- **Significance**: Most widely read history of the 18th century. Made Hume famous and financially independent.
- **Network Role**: Established Hume's contemporary reputation (more than his philosophy).

### A Philosophical Enquiry into the Sublime and Beautiful (1757)
- **ID**: `object-sublime-beautiful`
- **Type**: Book (aesthetics)
- **Creator**: Edmund Burke
- **Date**: 1757
- **Language**: English
- **Significance**: Influential theory of aesthetic experience. Distinguished sublime (terror, vastness) from beautiful (smooth, delicate).
- **Network Role**: Influenced Kant's aesthetics; Burke's early work before political writings.

### The Theory of Moral Sentiments (1759)
- **ID**: `object-theory-moral-sentiments`
- **Type**: Book (moral philosophy)
- **Creator**: Adam Smith
- **Date**: 1759
- **Language**: English
- **Significance**: Smith's moral philosophy based on sympathy and the "impartial spectator." Reveals Smith as more than economist.
- **Network Role**: Influenced by Hutcheson and Hume; Smith's first major work.

### History of Scotland (1759)
- **ID**: `object-history-scotland`
- **Type**: Book (history)
- **Creator**: William Robertson
- **Date**: 1759
- **Language**: English
- **Significance**: Established Robertson as major historian. Model of philosophical history.
- **Network Role**: Product of Edinburgh intellectual circles.

### Elements of Criticism (1762)
- **ID**: `object-elements-criticism`
- **Type**: Book (aesthetics/criticism)
- **Creator**: Henry Home, Lord Kames
- **Date**: 1762
- **Language**: English
- **Significance**: Systematic theory of aesthetic taste and literary criticism.
- **Network Role**: Influential in literary circles; showed range of Scottish thought.

### Inquiry into the Human Mind on the Principles of Common Sense (1764)
- **ID**: `object-inquiry-common-sense`
- **Type**: Book (philosophy)
- **Creator**: Thomas Reid
- **Date**: 1764
- **Language**: English
- **Significance**: Founded Common Sense philosophy as alternative to Hume's skepticism. Argued for reliable innate beliefs.
- **Network Role**: Major response to Hume; influenced American philosophy.

### Essay on the History of Civil Society (1767)
- **ID**: `object-essay-civil-society`
- **Type**: Book (social philosophy)
- **Creator**: Adam Ferguson
- **Date**: 1767
- **Language**: English
- **Significance**: Pioneer work of sociology. Analyzed stages of social development and dangers of commercial society.
- **Network Role**: Read by Marx; major contribution of Scottish social thought.

### An Inquiry into the Nature and Causes of the Wealth of Nations (1776)
- **ID**: `object-wealth-nations`
- **Type**: Book (economics)
- **Creator**: Adam Smith
- **Date**: 1776
- **Language**: English
- **Significance**: Foundation of classical economics. Analyzed division of labor, markets, and critique of mercantilism. "Invisible hand."
- **Network Role**: Influenced by Physiocrats; translated by Morellet; shaped modern economics.
- **External Links**: [Library of Economics and Liberty](https://www.econlib.org/library/Smith/smWN.html)

### Dialogues Concerning Natural Religion (1779)
- **ID**: `object-dialogues-natural-religion`
- **Type**: Book (philosophy of religion)
- **Creator**: David Hume
- **Date**: 1779 (posthumous)
- **Language**: English
- **Significance**: Devastating critique of arguments for God's existence, especially the design argument. Withheld during Hume's lifetime.
- **Network Role**: Adam Smith supervised publication; too dangerous to publish earlier.

### Reflections on the Revolution in France (1790)
- **ID**: `object-reflections-revolution`
- **Type**: Book (political philosophy)
- **Creator**: Edmund Burke
- **Date**: 1790
- **Language**: English
- **Significance**: Founding text of conservatism. Criticized French Revolution and abstract political theorizing.
- **Network Role**: Provoked Paine's *Rights of Man*; defined conservative-radical debate.

### Introduction to the Principles of Morals and Legislation (1789)
- **ID**: `object-principles-morals-legislation`
- **Type**: Book (philosophy/jurisprudence)
- **Creator**: Jeremy Bentham
- **Date**: 1789
- **Language**: English
- **Significance**: Systematic statement of utilitarianism. Developed "felicific calculus" for measuring pleasure and pain.
- **Network Role**: Built on Helvétius; foundation of utilitarian tradition.

## 4. German Works (1713-1790)

### Rational Thoughts on God, the World, and the Soul of Man (German Logic) (1713)
- **ID**: `object-german-logic`
- **Type**: Book (philosophy)
- **Creator**: Christian Wolff
- **Date**: 1713
- **Language**: German
- **Significance**: First major philosophical work in German (vs. Latin). Systematized rationalist philosophy for German universities.
- **Network Role**: Dominated German philosophy until Kant; basis of Kant's early training.

### Aesthetica (1750-58)
- **ID**: `object-aesthetica`
- **Type**: Book (philosophy)
- **Creator**: Alexander Gottlieb Baumgarten
- **Date**: 1750-58
- **Language**: Latin
- **Significance**: Coined the term "aesthetics" and founded it as a philosophical discipline. Study of sensory cognition.
- **Network Role**: Influenced Kant's *Critique of Judgment*.

### Metaphysica (1739)
- **ID**: `object-metaphysica`
- **Type**: Textbook (metaphysics)
- **Creator**: Alexander Gottlieb Baumgarten
- **Date**: 1739
- **Language**: Latin
- **Significance**: Standard metaphysics textbook. Kant used it for his lectures throughout his career.
- **Network Role**: Basis of Kant's university teaching.

### Laocoon: An Essay on the Limits of Painting and Poetry (1766)
- **ID**: `object-laocoon`
- **Type**: Essay (aesthetics)
- **Creator**: Gotthold Ephraim Lessing
- **Date**: 1766
- **Language**: German
- **Significance**: Distinguished temporal arts (poetry) from spatial arts (painting). Major contribution to aesthetic theory.
- **Network Role**: Influential in German aesthetic tradition; friend Mendelssohn contributed ideas.

### Phädon (1767)
- **ID**: `object-phadon`
- **Type**: Book (philosophy)
- **Creator**: Moses Mendelssohn
- **Date**: 1767
- **Language**: German
- **Significance**: Modern version of Plato's *Phaedo* arguing for immortality of the soul. Major popular success.
- **Network Role**: Established Mendelssohn's fame; basis for "German Socrates" epithet.

### Treatise on the Origin of Language (1772)
- **ID**: `object-treatise-origin-language`
- **Type**: Essay (philosophy of language)
- **Creator**: Johann Gottfried Herder
- **Date**: 1772
- **Language**: German
- **Significance**: Argued language emerged naturally from human reflection, not divine gift. Won Berlin Academy prize.
- **Network Role**: Showed Herder's move beyond Kantian framework.

### Nathan the Wise (1779)
- **ID**: `object-nathan-wise`
- **Type**: Drama
- **Creator**: Gotthold Ephraim Lessing
- **Date**: 1779
- **Language**: German
- **Significance**: Drama promoting religious tolerance through the "Ring Parable." Nathan modeled on Mendelssohn.
- **Network Role**: Monument of Enlightenment tolerance; product of Lessing-Mendelssohn friendship.

### Critique of Pure Reason (1781)
- **ID**: `object-critique-pure-reason`
- **Type**: Book (philosophy)
- **Creator**: Immanuel Kant
- **Date**: 1781 (2nd ed. 1787)
- **Language**: German
- **Significance**: Transformed philosophy. Synthesized rationalism and empiricism. Argued we can know only phenomena, not things-in-themselves.
- **Network Role**: Response to Hume's skepticism; foundation of German Idealism.
- **External Links**: [Kant's Gesammelte Schriften](https://korpora.zim.uni-duisburg-essen.de/kant/)

### Jerusalem, or On Religious Power and Judaism (1783)
- **ID**: `object-jerusalem`
- **Type**: Book (political philosophy/theology)
- **Creator**: Moses Mendelssohn
- **Date**: 1783
- **Language**: German
- **Significance**: Defense of Jewish practice and argument for separation of church and state. Major work of Jewish Enlightenment.
- **Network Role**: Statement of Haskalah principles.

### "What is Enlightenment?" (1784)
- **ID**: `object-what-is-enlightenment`
- **Type**: Essay
- **Creator**: Immanuel Kant
- **Date**: 1784
- **Language**: German
- **Significance**: Defining statement of Enlightenment ideals. "Sapere aude" (Dare to know). Argued Enlightenment is humanity's emergence from self-imposed immaturity.
- **Network Role**: Mendelssohn published similar essay same year; captures Enlightenment self-understanding.

### Ideas for the Philosophy of History of Humanity (1784-91)
- **ID**: `object-ideas-philosophy-history`
- **Type**: Book (philosophy of history)
- **Creator**: Johann Gottfried Herder
- **Date**: 1784-91
- **Language**: German
- **Significance**: Comprehensive philosophy of history emphasizing cultural diversity and development. Transitional toward Romanticism.
- **Network Role**: Showed limits of Enlightenment universalism; critiqued by Kant.

### Critique of Practical Reason (1788)
- **ID**: `object-critique-practical-reason`
- **Type**: Book (moral philosophy)
- **Creator**: Immanuel Kant
- **Date**: 1788
- **Language**: German
- **Significance**: Kant's systematic ethics. Categorical imperative, duty, and moral autonomy.
- **Network Role**: Influenced by Rousseau's ideas about moral freedom.

### Critique of Judgment (1790)
- **ID**: `object-critique-judgment`
- **Type**: Book (aesthetics/teleology)
- **Creator**: Immanuel Kant
- **Date**: 1790
- **Language**: German
- **Significance**: Third Critique. Analysis of aesthetic and teleological judgment. Bridged theoretical and practical philosophy.
- **Network Role**: Built on Baumgarten and Burke; influenced Romantic aesthetics.

## 5. Italian Works

### On Crimes and Punishments (Dei delitti e delle pene) (1764)
- **ID**: `object-crimes-punishments`
- **Type**: Book (legal philosophy)
- **Creator**: Cesare Beccaria
- **Date**: 1764
- **Language**: Italian
- **Significance**: Revolutionary critique of criminal justice. Opposed torture and death penalty. Promoted proportional punishment.
- **Network Role**: Commented on by Voltaire; influenced Bentham and Jefferson; translated across Europe.

## 6. American Works

### Experiments and Observations on Electricity (1751)
- **ID**: `object-experiments-electricity`
- **Type**: Book (natural philosophy)
- **Creator**: Benjamin Franklin
- **Date**: 1751
- **Language**: English
- **Significance**: Established Franklin's international scientific reputation. Reported kite experiment and theory of electrical fluid.
- **Network Role**: Made Franklin famous in Europe; basis for his reception in Paris.

### Common Sense (1776)
- **ID**: `object-common-sense`
- **Type**: Pamphlet (political philosophy)
- **Creator**: Thomas Paine
- **Date**: 1776
- **Language**: English
- **Significance**: Most widely read pamphlet of American Revolution. Made case for independence in accessible language.
- **Network Role**: Franklin helped Paine emigrate; shows Enlightenment ideas in revolutionary action.

### Declaration of Independence (1776)
