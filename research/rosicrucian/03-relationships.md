## Documented Relationships

### Tübingen Circle Relationships

#### Tobias Hess → Johann Valentin Andreae (Mentorship)
- **Type**: taught / influenced
- **Evidence**: "Andreae dedicated works to Hess and referred to him in glowing terms. De Christiani Cosmoxeni genitura (1612) was dedicated to Hess. Andreae wrote a memorial for Hess when he died in 1614."
- **Source**: Cambridge scholarship, Brill
- **Dates**: c. 1605-1614
- **Strength**: strong

#### Christoph Besold → Johann Valentin Andreae (Mentorship/Collaboration)
- **Type**: influenced / collaborated_with
- **Evidence**: "Andreae befriended Besold, who encouraged his interest in esotericism and helped shape his later involvement with the Rosicrucian manifestos. Besold also provided Andreae access to a large personal library."
- **Source**: Brill
- **Dates**: c. 1605-1638
- **Strength**: strong

#### Johannes Kepler ↔ Christoph Besold (Friendship)
- **Type**: corresponded_with / knows
- **Evidence**: "Besold studied jurisprudence and during the early 1590s was a close friend of Kepler. They were students at the University of Tübingen together. When Kepler's mother was accused of witchcraft in 1615, Besold acted as one of the jurists in her defense."
- **Source**: Multiple scholarly sources
- **Dates**: c. 1590-1630
- **Strength**: strong

#### Johannes Kepler → Michael Mästlin (Student-Teacher)
- **Type**: studied_under
- **Evidence**: Kepler studied under Mästlin at Tübingen; Mästlin introduced him to Copernican astronomy.
- **Source**: Widely documented
- **Dates**: c. 1589-1594
- **Strength**: strong

#### Johann Valentin Andreae → Michael Mästlin (Student-Teacher)
- **Type**: studied_under
- **Evidence**: "Andreae studied under Michael Mästlin (Kepler's teacher at Tübingen)"
- **Source**: Thonyc.wordpress.com (history of science blog)
- **Dates**: c. 1602-1608
- **Strength**: moderate (single secondary source)

#### Johann Arndt → Johann Valentin Andreae (Influence)
- **Type**: influenced
- **Evidence**: "Andreae highly praised True Christianity and saw in it a shift from formal orthodoxy toward 'praxis.' He published Christianismus genuinus (1615) as an extract or summary of Arndt's treatise."
- **Source**: Vdoc.pub (Pietist Theologians), Britannica
- **Dates**: c. 1605-1621
- **Strength**: strong

### Prague Court Circle Relationships

#### John Dee ↔ Edward Kelley (Collaboration)
- **Type**: collaborated_with
- **Evidence**: "Dee meets Kelley in 1582, beginning their spiritual conferences." They worked together on angelic communications until parting in 1589.
- **Source**: Wikipedia, multiple scholarly sources
- **Dates**: 1582-1589
- **Strength**: strong

#### Rudolf II → John Dee (Patronage)
- **Type**: patronized (custom relationship)
- **Evidence**: "Dee and Kelley traveled through Central Europe seeking patrons. They first made contact with Rudolf II in Prague in 1583."
- **Source**: Wikipedia
- **Dates**: 1583-1589
- **Strength**: moderate

#### Rudolf II → Edward Kelley (Patronage)
- **Type**: patronized
- **Evidence**: Kelley remained in Bohemia after Dee's departure and worked as an alchemist under Rudolf II's patronage.
- **Source**: Wikipedia
- **Dates**: 1589-1597
- **Strength**: moderate

#### Rudolf II → Michael Maier (Patronage/Employment)
- **Type**: employed
- **Evidence**: "On 19 September 1609 he formally entered the service of Emperor Rudolf II as his physician and counsellor Imperial. Shortly afterwards, he was elevated to hereditary nobility."
- **Source**: Wikipedia
- **Dates**: 1609-1611
- **Strength**: strong

#### Heinrich Khunrath ↔ John Dee (Met)
- **Type**: knows
- **Evidence**: "He met John Dee in Bremen on 27 May 1589. At that point, Dee was returning to England from Bohemia. Khunrath would later praise Dee in his writings."
- **Source**: Wikipedia
- **Dates**: 1589 (single meeting)
- **Strength**: strong (specific date documented)

#### Heinrich Khunrath ↔ Edward Kelley (Met)
- **Type**: knows
- **Evidence**: "Subsequently, Khunrath made his way to Rudolf II's court in Prague, where he also met Edward Kelley."
- **Source**: Wikipedia
- **Dates**: c. 1589-1591
- **Strength**: moderate

#### Vilém of Rožmberk → John Dee & Edward Kelley (Patronage)
- **Type**: patronized
- **Evidence**: Vilém hosted Dee and Kelley at his castle in Třeboň.
- **Source**: Wikipedia
- **Dates**: 1586-1589
- **Strength**: strong

#### Tycho Brahe → Johannes Kepler (Employment/Collaboration)
- **Type**: collaborated_with / employed
- **Evidence**: Kepler became Brahe's assistant in Prague in 1600 and inherited his observational data.
- **Source**: Widely documented
- **Dates**: 1600-1601
- **Strength**: strong

### English Rosicrucian Relationships

#### Robert Fludd ↔ Andreas Libavius (Intellectual Opposition)
- **Type**: opposed
- **Evidence**: "When the Rosicrucian manifestos appeared, Libavius published Analysis Confessionis Fraternitatis de Rosea Cruce in 1615. Fludd responded in multiple works beginning in 1616 (Apologia Compendiaria, Tractatus Apologeticus)."
- **Source**: Wikipedia, Brill, EBSCO
- **Dates**: 1615-1616
- **Strength**: strong

#### Thomas Vaughan → Fama/Confessio (Translator)
- **Type**: translated
- **Evidence**: "Thomas Vaughan, under his pseudonym Eugenius Philalethes, translated the Fama and Confessio into English in 1652."
- **Source**: Folger Shakespeare Library catalog
- **Dates**: 1652
- **Strength**: strong
- **Note**: This is person→object relationship

#### William Backhouse → Elias Ashmole (Initiation/Teaching)
- **Type**: taught
- **Evidence**: "Reputed to have initiated Ashmole into Rosicrucian knowledge."
- **Source**: Wikipedia
- **Dates**: c. 1651
- **Strength**: moderate (traditional account)

#### Thomas Vaughan ↔ Elias Ashmole (Association)
- **Type**: knows / associated_with
- **Evidence**: Both were active in English alchemical/Rosicrucian circles in the 1650s; both published major works in 1652.
- **Source**: Inferred from contemporary activity
- **Dates**: c. 1650-1666

### Additional Relationships (Resolving Orphan Nodes)

These relationships were added to ensure all enumerated persons have at least one connection in the network.

#### Sebastian Franck → Valentin Weigel (Intellectual Influence)
- **Type**: influenced
- **Evidence**: Franck's spiritualist writings on the "inner Word" and critique of institutional religion directly influenced Weigel's development of similar themes. Weigel explicitly drew on Franck's ideas about the primacy of inner spiritual experience.
- **Source**: Cambridge Handbook of Western Mysticism and Esotericism; Wikipedia (Weigel article)
- **Dates**: Posthumous influence (Franck d. 1542; Weigel active 1560s-1588)
- **Strength**: strong (intellectual influence)

#### Adam von Bodenstein → Paracelsus (Published Works Of)
- **Type**: published (custom)
- **Evidence**: "Published over 40 Paracelsian titles from 1560 onward." Bodenstein was the most important early publisher of Paracelsus's works, making them widely available after Paracelsus's death.
- **Source**: Wikipedia, Encyclopedia.com
- **Dates**: 1560-1577
- **Strength**: strong

#### Oswald Croll ↔ Rudolf II (Patronage/Court Connection)
- **Type**: associated_with
- **Evidence**: Croll was connected to Rudolf II's court in Prague and the court's alchemical circles. His *Basilica Chymica* (1609) was dedicated to Christian of Anhalt, a Protestant prince connected to the court milieu.
- **Source**: Wikipedia, Encyclopedia.com
- **Dates**: c. 1600-1609
- **Strength**: moderate

#### Daniel Mögling ↔ Johann Valentin Andreae (Intellectual Circle)
- **Type**: associated_with
- **Evidence**: Mögling was part of Andreae's wider intellectual circle and wrote Rosicrucian apologia (*Speculum Sophicum Rhodostauroticum*, 1618) that defended and elaborated on the manifesto themes.
- **Source**: Scholarly literature on Rosicrucianism
- **Dates**: c. 1615-1625
- **Strength**: moderate

#### Caspar Schwenckfeld → Karl Widemann (Works Collected By)
- **Type**: collected_by (custom)
- **Evidence**: "Over 30 years collected/copied large portions of Paracelsian corpus and works by Schwenckfeld, Krautwald." Widemann's manuscript collection included significant Schwenckfelder materials.
- **Source**: Research file (02-enumeration.md)
- **Dates**: c. 1580-1610
- **Strength**: moderate

#### Conrad Khunrath ↔ Heinrich Khunrath (Family)
- **Type**: related_to (siblings)
- **Evidence**: Conrad was the brother of Heinrich Khunrath. Both were involved in alchemy and Paracelsian medicine.
- **Source**: Wikipedia
- **Dates**: Lifelong (both born 1550s)
- **Strength**: strong

#### Tycho Brahe ↔ Rudolf II (Patronage/Employment)
- **Type**: patronized / employed
- **Evidence**: Brahe was appointed Imperial Court Astronomer by Rudolf II in 1599 and worked in Prague until his death in 1601.
- **Source**: Wikipedia, widely documented
- **Dates**: 1599-1601
- **Strength**: strong

#### Petrus Severinus → Oswald Croll (Intellectual Influence)
- **Type**: influenced
- **Evidence**: Severinus's *Idea Medicinae Philosophicae* (1571) provided the theoretical framework for systematic Paracelsianism that Croll built upon in his *Basilica Chymica*.
- **Source**: History of medicine scholarship
- **Dates**: Posthumous influence
- **Strength**: moderate

#### Daniel Sennert → Johannes Hartmann (Academic Contemporaries)
- **Type**: knows / associated_with
- **Evidence**: Both were academic physicians working to institutionalize chemical medicine in German universities during the same period. Sennert at Wittenberg and Hartmann at Marburg represented parallel efforts.
- **Source**: Academic history of medicine
- **Dates**: c. 1610-1631
- **Strength**: weak (inferred from parallel activity)

#### Raphael Eglin ↔ Benedictus Figulus (Network Connection)
- **Type**: knows
- **Evidence**: "Network included Figulus; preserved early Rosicrucian references." Eglin's alchemical handbook preserved social context and connections within the collector network.
- **Source**: Research file (02-enumeration.md)
- **Dates**: c. 1600-1619
- **Strength**: moderate
