# Rosicrucian-Paracelsian Network - Key Objects

**Date**: 2026-01-18  
**Purpose**: Document key works, manuscripts, and publications that connect individuals in the network.

---

## Rosicrucian Manifestos

### Fama Fraternitatis (1614)
- **Type**: manifesto
- **Creators**: Tübingen Circle (anonymous)
- **Date**: 1614 (first printed publication)
- **Language**: German (Latin editions followed)
- **Significance**: First Rosicrucian manifesto; announced the existence of a secret brotherhood and called for universal reformation
- **Connections**: Attributed to Andreae, Hess, Besold circle; responded to by Maier, Haslmayr, Fludd, Libavius

### Confessio Fraternitatis (1615)
- **Type**: manifesto
- **Creators**: Tübingen Circle (anonymous)
- **Date**: 1615
- **Language**: Latin
- **Significance**: Second manifesto; more explicitly theological and anti-papal
- **Connections**: Same authorship circle as Fama; translated by Thomas Vaughan (1652)

### Chymical Wedding of Christian Rosenkreutz (1616)
- **Type**: allegorical romance
- **Creator**: Johann Valentin Andreae
- **Date**: 1616
- **Language**: German
- **Significance**: Third manifesto; allegorical narrative of initiation; Andreae later admitted authorship
- **Connections**: Andreae acknowledged authorship; stylistically distinct from other manifestos

## Mystical-Theosophical Works

### Aurora (1612)
- **Type**: mystical treatise
- **Creator**: Jacob Boehme
- **Date**: 1612 (written; circulated in manuscript)
- **Language**: German
- **Significance**: Boehme's first major work; record of his mystical vision; suppressed by authorities
- **Connections**: Circulated despite church opposition; foundational for Boehmean tradition

### Mysterium Magnum (1623)
- **Type**: biblical commentary
- **Creator**: Jacob Boehme
- **Date**: 1623
- **Language**: German
- **Significance**: Boehme's commentary on Genesis; mature statement of his theosophy
- **Connections**: Later edited and published by Gichtel

### True Christianity (Vier Bücher vom wahren Christentum) (1605-1610)
- **Type**: devotional work
- **Creator**: Johann Arndt
- **Date**: 1605-1610 (published in parts)
- **Language**: German
- **Significance**: Major devotional work; shift from formal orthodoxy to lived spirituality; influenced Pietism
- **Connections**: Influenced Andreae; summarized by Andreae in Christianismus genuinus (1615)

### Christianopolis (1619)
- **Type**: utopia
- **Creator**: Johann Valentin Andreae
- **Date**: 1619
- **Language**: Latin
- **Significance**: Andreae's ideal Christian commonwealth; often compared to Bacon's New Atlantis
- **Connections**: Reflects Andreae's reform ideals after manifesto period

## Alchemical-Hermetic Works

### Amphitheatrum Sapientiae Aeternae (1595)
- **Type**: alchemical-mystical treatise
- **Creator**: Heinrich Khunrath
- **Date**: 1595 (expanded 1609)
- **Language**: Latin/German
- **Significance**: Major work of spiritual alchemy with famous emblematic engravings
- **Connections**: Khunrath met Dee; influenced Rosicrucian visual language

### Atalanta Fugiens (1617)
- **Type**: emblem book
- **Creator**: Michael Maier
- **Date**: 1617
- **Language**: Latin
- **Significance**: Masterpiece of alchemical emblems with musical fugues; 50 emblems with commentary
- **Connections**: Maier's most famous work; published after leaving Rudolf II's service

### Basilica Chymica (1609)
- **Type**: Paracelsian medicine
- **Creator**: Oswald Croll
- **Date**: 1609
- **Language**: Latin
- **Significance**: Major Paracelsian medical text; widely influential chemical pharmacy
- **Connections**: Croll connected to Prague court; work dedicated to Christian of Anhalt

### Utriusque Cosmi Historia (1617-1621)
- **Type**: cosmological treatise
- **Creator**: Robert Fludd
- **Date**: 1617-1621 (published in parts)
- **Language**: Latin
- **Significance**: Massive illustrated work on macrocosm and microcosm; Hermetic-Rosicrucian cosmology
- **Connections**: Fludd's major work; provoked response from Kepler

### Monas Hieroglyphica (1564)
- **Type**: Hermetic symbol treatise
- **Creator**: John Dee
- **Date**: 1564
- **Language**: Latin
- **Significance**: Dee's mysterious hieroglyph synthesizing alchemical and cosmological symbolism
- **Connections**: Earlier than Rosicrucian period but influential on later Hermeticism

## Polemical Works

### Apologia Compendiaria (1616)
- **Type**: Rosicrucian defense
- **Creator**: Robert Fludd
- **Date**: 1616
- **Language**: Latin
- **Significance**: First defense of the Rosicrucian manifestos
- **Connections**: Response to initial criticism; followed by Tractatus Apologeticus (1617)

### Analysis Confessionis Fraternitatis de Rosea Cruce (1615)
- **Type**: anti-Rosicrucian critique
- **Creator**: Andreas Libavius
- **Date**: 1615
- **Language**: Latin
- **Significance**: Major critique of Rosicrucian manifestos from an establishment chemist
- **Connections**: Provoked responses from Fludd

### Alchymia (1597)
- **Type**: chemistry textbook
- **Creator**: Andreas Libavius
- **Date**: 1597
- **Language**: Latin
- **Significance**: First systematic chemistry textbook; opposed mystical alchemy while accepting transmutation
- **Connections**: Libavius's reputation rested on this work

## Anthologies and Collections

### Theatrum Chemicum Britannicum (1652)
- **Type**: anthology
- **Creator**: Elias Ashmole
- **Date**: 1652
- **Language**: English
- **Significance**: Major collection of English alchemical texts; preserved many otherwise lost works
- **Connections**: Ashmole's major editorial achievement; preserves Rosicrucian-adjacent material

## Hartlib Circle Publications

### Pansophiae Prodromus (1639)
- **Type**: philosophical treatise
- **Creator**: Jan Amos Comenius
- **Date**: 1639 (published in London by Hartlib)
- **Language**: Latin
- **Significance**: Blueprint for Comenius's system of universal wisdom (pansophia); foundational text for the Hartlib Circle's educational reform agenda
- **Connections**: Published with Hartlib's assistance; influenced the entire reform movement; outlines integration of senses, reason, and revelation

### Via Lucis (1641/1668)
- **Type**: philosophical treatise
- **Creator**: Jan Amos Comenius
- **Date**: Written 1641; published 1668
- **Language**: Latin
- **Significance**: "The Way of Light" - proposed a universal college and universal language for spreading knowledge
- **Connections**: Written during Comenius's visit to England at Hartlib's invitation; dedicated to Royal Society

### Didactica Magna (c. 1633-1638)
- **Type**: educational treatise
- **Creator**: Jan Amos Comenius
- **Date**: c. 1633-1638 (Latin edition 1657)
- **Language**: Czech/Latin
- **Significance**: "The Great Didactic" - comprehensive theory of education for all; foundational work in pedagogical theory
- **Connections**: Core of Comenius's pansophic educational program; influenced by and influenced Hartlib Circle

### The Holy Guide (1662)
- **Type**: Rosicrucian treatise
- **Creator**: John Heydon
- **Date**: 1662
- **Language**: English
- **Significance**: Six-book work fusing Rosicrucian medicine, alchemy, and Baconian science; preface borrows from Bacon's New Atlantis
- **Connections**: Heydon linked Bacon's Salomon's House to Rosicrucian Temple; represents English popularization of Rosicrucian ideas

### The Reformed School (1648)
- **Type**: educational treatise
- **Creator**: John Dury (with Samuel Hartlib)
- **Date**: 1648
- **Language**: English
- **Significance**: Educational manifesto proposing practical reform of schooling
- **Connections**: Collaborative work of Hartlib and Dury; part of larger reform program

### The Reformed Librarie-Keeper (1650)
- **Type**: treatise on library science
- **Creator**: John Dury
- **Date**: 1650
- **Language**: English
- **Significance**: Early work on library organization and the role of librarians; pioneered concepts of library science
- **Connections**: Part of Hartlib Circle's vision for organized knowledge; influenced later information management
