# Rosicrucian-Paracelsian Network - Key Locations

**Date**: 2026-01-18  
**Purpose**: Document significant places where network members lived, worked, and connected.

---

## Universities and Academic Centers

### Tübingen
- **Type**: university / city
- **Country**: Württemberg (Holy Roman Empire)
- **Coordinates**: 48.52°N, 9.06°E
- **Significance**: University of Tübingen was the origin point of the Rosicrucian manifestos. The "Tübingen Circle" of Andreae, Hess, Besold, and others studied and worked here.
- **Key Figures**: Andreae, Hess, Besold, Kepler (student), Mästlin (professor)
- **Period**: c. 1580-1620

### Marburg
- **Type**: university / city
- **Country**: Hesse-Kassel (Holy Roman Empire)
- **Coordinates**: 50.81°N, 8.77°E
- **Significance**: Site of first university chemistry professorship (1609); under patronage of Moritz of Hesse-Kassel, became a center for Paracelsian medicine.
- **Key Figures**: Hartmann (professor), Petraeus, Eglin
- **Period**: 1609-1631

### Wittenberg
- **Type**: university / city
- **Country**: Saxony (Holy Roman Empire)
- **Coordinates**: 51.87°N, 12.65°E
- **Significance**: Major Lutheran university; Sennert's professorship bridged Galenic and Paracelsian medicine.
- **Key Figures**: Sennert, Bodenstein (nearby)
- **Period**: c. 1570-1640

## Courts and Political Centers

### Prague
- **Type**: imperial court / city
- **Country**: Bohemia (Holy Roman Empire)
- **Coordinates**: 50.08°N, 14.43°E
- **Significance**: Rudolf II's court was the most important center for esoteric thought in Central Europe. Attracted alchemists, astrologers, and natural philosophers from across Europe.
- **Key Figures**: Rudolf II, Dee, Kelley, Brahe, Kepler, Maier, Khunrath, Croll
- **Period**: 1576-1612 (Rudolf's reign)

### Heidelberg
- **Type**: electoral court / city
- **Country**: Palatinate (Holy Roman Empire)
- **Coordinates**: 49.40°N, 8.69°E
- **Significance**: Court of Frederick V, Elector Palatine. The "Rosicrucian hope" was partly invested in Frederick's Protestant court. Site of the Hortus Palatinus.
- **Key Figures**: Frederick V, Elizabeth Stuart, de Caus, Maier (visited)
- **Period**: 1610-1620 (until Frederick's exile)

### Kassel
- **Type**: landgrave court / city
- **Country**: Hesse-Kassel (Holy Roman Empire)
- **Coordinates**: 51.32°N, 9.50°E
- **Significance**: Moritz of Hesse-Kassel was a major patron of Paracelsian medicine and esoteric publication. The Fama was first published here (1614).
- **Key Figures**: Moritz of Hesse-Kassel, connections to Hartmann
- **Period**: c. 1600-1630

### The Hague
- **Type**: court-in-exile / city
- **Country**: Dutch Republic
- **Coordinates**: 52.08°N, 4.31°E
- **Significance**: Frederick V and Elizabeth Stuart's court in exile after the Battle of White Mountain (1620). Maintained intellectual and cultural activity.
- **Key Figures**: Frederick V, Elizabeth Stuart
- **Period**: 1621-1632

## Cities of Intellectual Activity

### Görlitz
- **Type**: city
- **Country**: Upper Lusatia / Silesia (Holy Roman Empire)
- **Coordinates**: 51.15°N, 14.99°E
- **Significance**: Jacob Boehme's home city. He lived here as a shoemaker and had his mystical experiences. Despite church opposition, his circle gathered here.
- **Key Figures**: Boehme
- **Period**: c. 1600-1624

### London
- **Type**: city
- **Country**: England
- **Coordinates**: 51.51°N, -0.13°E
- **Significance**: Center of English Rosicrucianism and Hermeticism. Fludd practiced medicine here; the Royal Society (later) had connections to this milieu.
- **Key Figures**: Fludd, Dee (earlier), Ashmole, Vaughan
- **Period**: c. 1580-1660

### Augsburg
- **Type**: city
- **Country**: Holy Roman Empire
- **Coordinates**: 48.37°N, 10.90°E
- **Significance**: Major center for manuscript collecting and book trade. Karl Widemann conducted much of his collecting activity here.
- **Key Figures**: Widemann
- **Period**: c. 1580-1620

### Basel
- **Type**: university / publishing center
- **Country**: Swiss Confederation
- **Coordinates**: 47.56°N, 7.59°E
- **Significance**: Major publishing center for Paracelsian texts. Bodenstein published many Paracelsian works from here.
- **Key Figures**: Bodenstein, Paracelsus (taught briefly)
- **Period**: c. 1530-1580

## Estates and Smaller Centers

### Třeboň (Wittingau)
- **Type**: castle / estate
- **Country**: Bohemia (Holy Roman Empire)
- **Coordinates**: 49.00°N, 14.77°E
- **Significance**: Estate of Vilém of Rožmberk, who hosted Dee and Kelley. Site of their alchemical work in Bohemia.
- **Key Figures**: Vilém of Rožmberk, Dee, Kelley
- **Period**: 1586-1589

## Location Network Notes

Key geographic patterns:
- **German-speaking universities**: Tübingen, Marburg, Wittenberg formed an academic network
- **Court patronage axis**: Prague → Heidelberg → Kassel supported esoteric thought
- **Publishing centers**: Basel, Frankfurt (not documented), Kassel for esoteric texts
- **English reception**: London as the main center
- **Exile networks**: The Hague after 1620
