## Research Institutes

### MILA (Montreal Institute for Learning Algorithms)
- **Type**: Research institute
- **ID**: `location-mila`
- **Address**: Montreal, Quebec, Canada
- **Founded**: 1993 (as LISA lab); renamed MILA 2017
- **Significance**: Largest academic AI research institute in world; Yoshua Bengio's creation
- **Key Figures**: Yoshua Bengio (founder), Aaron Courville, Doina Precup, Joelle Pineau
- **Notes**: Over 1000 researchers; partnership with Université de Montréal and McGill

### Vector Institute
- **Type**: Research institute
- **ID**: `location-vector-institute`
- **Address**: Toronto, Ontario, Canada
- **Founded**: 2017
- **Significance**: AI research institute focused on applied AI; Geoffrey Hinton co-founder
- **Key Figures**: Geoffrey Hinton (co-founder)
- **Notes**: Partnership with industry and universities; Toronto AI ecosystem anchor

### Stanford HAI (Human-Centered AI)
- **Type**: Research institute
- **ID**: `location-stanford-hai`
- **Address**: Stanford, California, USA
- **Founded**: 2019
- **Significance**: Research institute focused on human-centered AI and policy
- **Key Figures**: Fei-Fei Li (co-director), Percy Liang (director)
- **Notes**: AI Index report; policy research

### DAIR Institute
- **Type**: Research institute
- **ID**: `location-dair`
- **Address**: Remote/distributed
- **Founded**: 2021
- **Significance**: Independent AI ethics research founded by Timnit Gebru
- **Key Figures**: Timnit Gebru (founder)
- **Notes**: Founded after Gebru's departure from Google

### Amii (Alberta Machine Intelligence Institute)
- **Type**: Research institute
- **ID**: `location-amii`
- **Address**: Edmonton, Alberta, Canada
- **Founded**: 2002
- **Significance**: RL research; connects University of Alberta to industry
- **Key Figures**: Richard Sutton
- **Notes**: Part of Canadian AI strategy alongside MILA and Vector

## Geographic Distribution Notes

**By Region:**
- **North America**: San Francisco, Mountain View, Menlo Park, Seattle, New York, Pittsburgh, Berkeley, Toronto, Montreal, Edmonton (majority of activity)
- **Europe**: London, Paris, Munich, Oxford, Amsterdam
- **Asia**: Tokyo (limited; Chinese AI explicitly excluded)

**Concentration:**
- San Francisco Bay Area dominates with OpenAI, Anthropic, xAI, and proximity to Google/Meta
- Canadian corridor (Toronto-Montreal-Edmonton) is second major hub
- London is European AI capital (DeepMind)
- Paris emerging as European challenger (Mistral, FAIR Paris)
