## Major AI Labs (Private)

### OpenAI
- **Type**: AI research company
- **ID**: `entity-openai`
- **Founded**: December 2015
- **Headquarters**: San Francisco, California
- **Status**: Active (capped-profit since 2019)
- **Founders**: Sam Altman, Elon Musk, Greg Brockman, Ilya Sutskever, Wojciech Zaremba, John Schulman, Trevor Blackwell, Vicki Cheung, Pamela Vagata, Durk Kingma, Andrej Karpathy
- **Key Figures**: Sam Altman (CEO), Greg Brockman (President), Mira Murati (CTO, until 2024), Ilya Sutskever (Chief Scientist, until 2024)
- **Major Works**: GPT series, ChatGPT, DALL-E, Codex, CLIP
- **Description**: Founded as AI safety-focused non-profit, transitioned to "capped-profit" in 2019. Most influential AI lab in consumer AI; created ChatGPT which brought AI to mainstream.
- **Significance**: Creator of GPT models and ChatGPT; sparked the LLM revolution; center of AI commercialization
- **Relationships**: 
  - Elon Musk (co-founder, departed 2018)
  - Anthropic (rival, founded by OpenAI departures)
  - Microsoft (investor and partner)
- **Notes**: November 2023 board crisis briefly ousted Altman; major departures in 2024 (Sutskever, Murati, Leike)

### Anthropic
- **Type**: AI safety company
- **ID**: `entity-anthropic`
- **Founded**: 2021
- **Headquarters**: San Francisco, California
- **Status**: Active
- **Founders**: Dario Amodei, Daniela Amodei, Tom Brown, Jared Kaplan, Sam McCandlish, Chris Olah, Jack Clark
- **Key Figures**: Dario Amodei (CEO), Daniela Amodei (President), Jared Kaplan (Chief Science Officer)
- **Major Works**: Claude, Constitutional AI
- **Description**: AI safety-focused company founded by former OpenAI researchers concerned about safety prioritization. Developed Constitutional AI approach.
- **Significance**: Primary OpenAI competitor; leading voice in AI safety; creator of Claude
- **Relationships**:
  - OpenAI (origin; competitive relationship)
  - Google (investor)
  - Amazon (investor and cloud partner)
- **Notes**: Founded in response to OpenAI's commercialization direction; Jan Leike joined from OpenAI in 2024

### Google DeepMind
- **Type**: AI research lab (corporate)
- **ID**: `entity-google-deepmind`
- **Founded**: April 2023 (merger); DeepMind 2010, Google Brain 2011
- **Headquarters**: London (DeepMind), Mountain View (former Brain)
- **Status**: Active
- **Founders**: 
  - DeepMind: Demis Hassabis, Shane Legg, Mustafa Suleyman (2010)
  - Google Brain: Jeff Dean, Andrew Ng, Greg Corrado (2011)
- **Key Figures**: Demis Hassabis (CEO), Jeff Dean (Chief Scientist), Shane Legg (Chief AGI Scientist)
- **Major Works**: AlphaGo, AlphaFold, AlphaZero, Gemini, TensorFlow
- **Description**: Google's unified AI research organization formed by merging DeepMind and Google Brain in 2023. Combined the scientific research excellence of DeepMind with Google Brain's scale.
- **Significance**: Created landmark AI systems (AlphaGo, AlphaFold); now developing Gemini to compete with GPT-4
- **Relationships**:
  - Google/Alphabet (parent company)
  - OpenAI (competitor)
- **Notes**: DeepMind acquired by Google for ~$500M in 2014; 2024 Nobel Prizes for Hassabis and Jumper

### Meta AI / FAIR
- **Type**: AI research lab (corporate)
- **ID**: `entity-meta-ai`
- **Founded**: December 2013 (as Facebook AI Research)
- **Headquarters**: Menlo Park, CA; Paris, France; New York, NY
- **Status**: Active
- **Founders**: Yann LeCun
- **Key Figures**: Yann LeCun (Chief AI Scientist), Joelle Pineau (VP Research), Hugo Touvron
- **Major Works**: LLaMA, LLaMA 2, LLaMA 3, PyTorch (indirectly), fastText
- **Description**: Meta's AI research division. Strongly advocates for open AI research. Released LLaMA as open-weight models, catalyzing open-source AI ecosystem.
- **Significance**: Leading advocate for open AI; LLaMA release was pivotal moment for AI democratization
- **Relationships**:
  - Meta/Facebook (parent company)
  - Mistral AI (founded by former Meta researchers)
  - PyTorch Foundation
- **Notes**: LeCun frequently debates AI safety concerns publicly; LLaMA weights were leaked then officially released

### xAI
- **Type**: AI company
- **ID**: `entity-xai`
- **Founded**: July 2023
- **Headquarters**: San Francisco Bay Area
- **Status**: Active
- **Founders**: Elon Musk (founder), Igor Babuschkin, Christian Szegedy, Yuhuai Wu, and others
- **Key Figures**: Elon Musk (founder/CEO), Igor Babuschkin (departed 2025)

- **Notes**: Recruited heavily from DeepMind and Google; released Grok-1 weights as open source

## European AI Companies

### Mistral AI
- **Type**: AI company
- **ID**: `entity-mistral`
- **Founded**: April 2023
- **Headquarters**: Paris, France
- **Status**: Active
- **Founders**: Arthur Mensch (ex-DeepMind), Guillaume Lample (ex-Meta), Timothée Lacroix (ex-Meta)
- **Key Figures**: Arthur Mensch (CEO), Guillaume Lample (Chief Scientist), Timothée Lacroix (CTO)

- **Type**: AI company
- **ID**: `entity-stability`
- **Founded**: 2019
- **Headquarters**: London, UK
- **Status**: Active (turbulent)
- **Founders**: Emad Mostaque (departed March 2024)
- **Key Figures**: Emad Mostaque (founder, departed)

### Hugging Face
- **Type**: AI platform company
- **ID**: `entity-huggingface`
- **Founded**: 2016
- **Headquarters**: New York, NY; Paris, France
- **Status**: Active
- **Founders**: Clément Delangue, Julien Chaumond, Thomas Wolf
- **Key Figures**: Clément Delangue (CEO), Thomas Wolf (CSO), Margaret Mitchell (Chief AI Ethics Scientist)
- **Major Works**: Transformers library, Hugging Face Hub, Model Cards
- **Description**: Platform company providing tools and infrastructure for sharing and using AI models. Created the dominant Transformers library.
- **Significance**: Democratized access to AI models; central platform for open-source AI; hosts most publicly available models
- **Relationships**:
  - All major AI labs (model hosting)
  - PyTorch (primary framework integration)
- **Notes**: Valued at $4.5B; transitioned from chatbot startup to AI platform; Margaret Mitchell joined after Google departure

## Movements and Schools of Thought

### Toronto School of Deep Learning
- **Type**: Intellectual movement
- **ID**: `entity-toronto-school`
- **Founded**: ~2006 (deep learning revival)
- **Key Figures**: Geoffrey Hinton
- **Description**: Network of researchers trained by or influenced by Geoffrey Hinton at University of Toronto. Associated with backpropagation revival, deep belief networks, and practical deep learning.
- **Significance**: Trained many key AI figures including Ilya Sutskever, Alex Krizhevsky
- **Notes**: AlexNet emerged from this school

### Montreal School of Deep Learning
- **Type**: Intellectual movement
- **ID**: `entity-montreal-school`
- **Founded**: ~2000s
- **Key Figures**: Yoshua Bengio
- **Description**: Network of researchers associated with MILA and Yoshua Bengio. Associated with GANs, attention mechanisms, representation learning.
- **Significance**: Ian Goodfellow invented GANs here; foundational NLP research
- **Notes**: Close collaboration with Toronto School

### AI Safety / Alignment Movement
- **Type**: Intellectual movement
- **ID**: `entity-ai-safety-movement`
- **Founded**: ~2010s (modern form)
- **Key Figures**: Dario Amodei, Jan Leike, Chris Olah, Geoffrey Hinton (recent)
- **Description**: Movement focused on ensuring advanced AI systems are safe and aligned with human values.
- **Significance**: Drives safety research at Anthropic, OpenAI superalignment team; growing influence
- **Notes**: Hinton became prominent safety voice after leaving Google in 2023

### Scaling Hypothesis
- **Type**: Research paradigm
- **ID**: `entity-scaling-hypothesis`
- **Founded**: ~2020
- **Key Figures**: Jared Kaplan, Sam McCandlish, Dario Amodei
- **Description**: Theory that AI capabilities scale predictably with compute, data, and model size according to power laws.
- **Significance**: Theoretical foundation for GPT-3/4 scale models; drives AI compute race
- **Notes**: Scaling laws paper by future Anthropic founders

### Open Source AI / Open Weights Movement
- **Type**: Movement
- **ID**: `entity-open-ai-movement`
- **Key Figures**: Yann LeCun, Clément Delangue, Hugo Touvron
- **Description**: Movement advocating for open release of AI model weights and research.
- **Significance**: LLaMA release, Hugging Face ecosystem, Mistral models
- **Notes**: Tension with safety-focused approaches that favor closed models
