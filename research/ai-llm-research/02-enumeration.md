## Deep Learning Pioneers ("Godfathers of AI")

The three researchers who received the 2018 Turing Award for foundational work on deep learning.

### Geoffrey Hinton (1947-)
- **Role**: Computer scientist, cognitive psychologist; "Godfather of AI"
- **Birth**: December 6, 1947, London, England
- **Location**: Toronto, Canada; formerly Google Brain
- **Significance**: Pioneer of neural networks and deep learning; co-inventor of backpropagation; inventor of Boltzmann machines; trained many key figures including Ilya Sutskever
- **Key Works**: Backpropagation paper (1986 with Rumelhart & Williams), Deep belief networks, AlexNet (with Krizhevsky & Sutskever)
- **Awards**: Turing Award (2018), Nobel Prize in Physics (2024)
- **Connections**: Trained Ilya Sutskever, Alex Krizhevsky, Ruslan Salakhutdinov, Yee-Whye Teh; collaborated with Bengio and LeCun; joined Google Brain 2013, resigned 2023
- **Notes**: Left Google in 2023 to speak freely about AI risks; co-founded Vector Institute (2017)

### Yoshua Bengio (1964-)
- **Role**: Computer scientist; founder of MILA
- **Birth**: March 5, 1964, Paris, France
- **Location**: Montreal, Canada (Université de Montréal)
- **Significance**: Pioneer of deep learning; foundational work on word embeddings, sequence modeling, generative models
- **Key Works**: Neural probabilistic language models, GAN research (with Goodfellow), attention mechanisms
- **Awards**: Turing Award (2018), Knight of Legion of Honor (2022)
- **Connections**: Trained Ian Goodfellow, supervised Aaron Courville; collaborated with Hinton and LeCun; founded MILA
- **Notes**: Founded MILA (Montreal Institute for Learning Algorithms); co-founded LawZero (2025) for AI safety

### Yann LeCun (1960-)
- **Role**: Chief AI Scientist at Meta; computer scientist
- **Birth**: 1960, Paris, France
- **Location**: New York (Meta AI); Paris (FAIR)
- **Significance**: Pioneer of convolutional neural networks; foundational work on image recognition
- **Key Works**: LeNet (1989), convolutional neural networks
- **Awards**: Turing Award (2018)
- **Connections**: Founded Meta AI/FAIR (2013); collaborated with Hinton and Bengio; trained numerous researchers
- **Notes**: Vocal advocate for open AI research; frequently debates AI safety concerns publicly
