## 7. Contested or Debated Claims

### Historical Debates

#### 1. Shannon-Wiener Priority

**Issue**: Who deserves more credit for information theory/cybernetics?
- Shannon published "Mathematical Theory of Communication" in 1948
- Wiener published "Cybernetics" also in 1948
- Some parallel development; some mutual influence

**Resolution**: Both credited appropriately; relationship marked as bidirectional influence

#### 2. ENIAC Attribution Controversy

**Issue**: Von Neumann's EDVAC report didn't credit Mauchly and Eckert
- Led to decades of dispute about stored-program concept origins
- Goldstine and von Neumann vs. Eckert-Mauchly

**Resolution**: All parties included; relationships documented as collaborative

#### 3. Perceptrons Controversy

**Issue**: Did Minsky-Papert's "Perceptrons" (1969) unfairly kill neural network research?
- Some argue the critique was mathematically correct but misleading about potential
- Multi-layer networks could overcome limitations but funding dried up

**Resolution**: Relationship marked as "opposed"; note added about historical debate

#### 4. Wiener-Pitts Break

**Issue**: Cause of Wiener's break with Pitts (~1952)
- Various accounts involving Wiener's wife Margaret
- Pitts became isolated and alcoholic

**Resolution**: Noted in relationships as deterioration; causes not specified as debated
